## 한줄요약
체계적 문헌고찰 (50편, 단일치 상악 전치/소구치 임플란트): 즉시식립 (Type 1, Immediate)은 1–3년 시점에 1 mm 이상 협측 변연 점막 퇴축 (Midfacial Recession)이 사이트의 9–41% (중앙값 26%)에서 발생하고, 콘빔 컴퓨터단층촬영 (Cone-Beam Computed Tomography, CBCT)에서 협측 골판 (Facial Bone Wall)이 36–57% 사이트에서 감지되지 않은 반면, 조기식립 (Type 2/3, Early)과 윤곽 증대술 (Contour Augmentation) 병행 시 90% 이상에서 골판이 유지되고 1 mm 이상 퇴축은 보고되지 않음.

## Summary
This **ITI Consensus systematic review** (50 included studies; 6 RCTs, 6 cohort, 5 cross-sectional, 33 case series) compared esthetic outcomes between **immediate (type 1)** and **early (type 2 / type 3)** implant placement in the maxillary anterior and premolar regions. Heterogeneity precluded meta-analysis. Across 8 studies of type 1, **9–41% of sites had >1 mm midfacial mucosal recession at 1–3 years (median 26%)**; in 2 studies of type 2/3 placement, **no sites had >1 mm recession**. CBCT data showed that the **facial bone wall was undetectable in 36–57% of type 1 grafted sites** — those sites had worse recession — whereas type 2/3 with simultaneous **contour augmentation (GBR)** preserved a detectable facial wall in >90% of cases. Modern type 1 protocols therefore impose strict case selection (thick biotype, intact facial wall) to reduce esthetic risk.

## Key Contributions
- Quantified the **esthetic-risk premium** of type 1 over type 2/3 placement at the level of objective indices and CBCT bone presence.
- Established benchmark figures still cited: **~26% median recession rate** for type 1; **>90% facial bone preservation** for early + contour augmentation.
- Anchored ITI consensus on selection criteria (thick biotype, intact buccal plate) for immediate placement.
- Highlighted absence of evidence for multi-tooth immediate placement and identified the facial-bone–mucosal-stability relationship as the key unresolved question.

## Methodology
- Electronic + manual literature search; all levels of evidence accepted; case series required ≥5 cases.
- 1,686 titles → 114 full text → **50 included studies**.
- Outcomes: PES and other esthetic indices, positional change of midfacial mucosa, CBCT detection of facial bone.
- Stratified by ITI placement-timing classification (type 1 immediate; type 2/3 early; type 4 late).
- Qualitative synthesis; heterogeneity in design and follow-up precluded meta-analysis.

## Results
- Type 1: 8 studies show 9–41% of sites with >1 mm midfacial recession (median 26%) at 1–3 years.
- Type 2/3: 2 studies show no sites with >1 mm recession.
- Facial bone undetectable on CBCT in 36% and 57% of type 1 grafted sites — these sites had more recession.
- Facial bone visible in >90% of type 2/3 sites with simultaneous GBR contour augmentation.
- No studies on multi-tooth immediate placement identified.
