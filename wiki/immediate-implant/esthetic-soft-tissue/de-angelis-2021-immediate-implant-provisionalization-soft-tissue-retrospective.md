## Summary

This 1-year retrospective study of 48 immediately placed and provisionalized anterior single implants compared three soft-tissue strategies: subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG), xenogenic collagen matrix (XCM), and no graft (NG, control). All implants survived. Facial soft-tissue thickness and buccal marginal level were preserved comparably by SCTG and XCM, whereas the no-graft group lost significantly more of both. The findings support soft-tissue augmentation at immediate implants and position XCM as a viable, donor-site-free alternative to autogenous CTG.

## Key Contributions

- Three-arm clinical comparison of SCTG vs XCM vs no graft at IIPP sites.
- Supports XCM as an alternative to autogenous CTG for facial tissue stability.

## Methodology

- 48 anterior single implants, type 1 placement + provisionalization; arms SCTG / XCM / NG.
- 1-year outcomes: soft-tissue marginal change, FSTT, peri-implant health, esthetics, satisfaction.

## Results

- Survival 100% all groups.
- FSTT: SCTG ≈ XCM (P > 0.05); NG significantly worse.
- Buccal marginal level: NG worse than both grafted groups (P < 0.05).
