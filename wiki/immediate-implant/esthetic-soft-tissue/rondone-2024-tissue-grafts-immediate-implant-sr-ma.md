## Summary
Systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the efficacy of hard and/or soft tissue grafts combined with type-1 (immediate) implant placement. PICO-based strategy; Universidade Católica Portuguesa, Viseu, Portugal. Published January 2024.

Primary outcomes: implant survival rate, pocket depth, marginal peri-implant recession, bone loss, bone thickness (volumetric change), interproximal bone level, papilla migration, radiographic evaluation. Secondary outcomes: Pink Esthetic Score (PES), Implant Stability Quotient (ISQ), VAS, biological complications.

Tissue grafts associated with improved ISQ values, reduced marginal recession, better volumetric bone preservation, and improved PES vs non-grafted controls. Combined hard + soft tissue grafts showed strongest esthetic benefits. High heterogeneity across graft material types limits pooled conclusions.

## Key Contributions
- Tissue grafts improve all key immediate implant outcome measures: stability (ISQ), esthetics (PES), soft tissue margin (recession), bone preservation
- Combined hard + soft grafts: best esthetic outcomes — synergistic effect on PES
- Gap filling (between implant surface and socket wall) and connective tissue graft together maximize esthetic outcomes in immediate implant cases
- Limitation: graft material heterogeneity (xenograft, allograft, CTG, collagen matrix) is so high that specific material recommendations cannot be made from available data

## Methodology
- **Design**: SR+MA following PICO
- **Primary outcomes**: Survival rate, PD, recession, bone loss, volumetric change, interproximal bone, papilla, radiographic
- **Secondary outcomes**: PES, ISQ, VAS, biological complications
- **Meta-analysis**: Conducted for outcomes with sufficient data

## Results

| Outcome | Direction with Graft |
|---|---|
| ISQ (implant stability) | Improved vs no graft |
| Marginal recession | Reduced |
| Volumetric bone preservation | Better |
| PES (Pink Esthetic Score) | Improved |
| Biological complications | Variable; generally favorable |

Combined hard + soft tissue graft: superior esthetic outcomes (PES) vs graft type alone
