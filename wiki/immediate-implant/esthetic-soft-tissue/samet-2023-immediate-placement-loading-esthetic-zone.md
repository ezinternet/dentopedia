## Summary
Loss of an anterior tooth is uniquely traumatic for patients because anterior teeth shape facial esthetics and personal identity. Two post-extraction phenomena complicate restoration: papilla flattening/loss and three-dimensional bone resorption (horizontal and vertical). This 2023 IntechOpen book chapter by Samet provides a clinically grounded narrative on **immediate implant placement and loading in the esthetic zone** — when it works, why it works, and how to execute it.

The argument is biological: placing an implant immediately after extraction can **reduce** post-extraction bone loss compared with delayed placement, particularly in well-selected cases (intact buccal plate, thick biotype, no apical pathology). Combined with an immediate, out-of-occlusion provisional shaped as an ovate pontic, the technique preserves both the **buccal bone** ("white") and the **gingival architecture** ("pink"). The socket-shield technique is acknowledged as effective in skilled hands but case-restricted.

Case selection is non-negotiable. Thin or fenestrated buccal plates, active periapical infection, and severe biotype thinness shift the balance toward early or delayed placement with ridge preservation.

## Key Contributions
- Practical decision flow for the esthetic zone: diagnosis → atraumatic extraction → palatal-axis implant placement with apical + palatal primary stability → graft into gap → connective-tissue graft (when indicated) → immediate ovate-pontic provisional out of occlusion.
- Frames immediate implant as a **bone- and soft-tissue-preserving** procedure rather than a shortcut.
- Sets realistic patient expectations: midfacial recession ~0.5–1 mm and modest papilla changes are typical even in well-executed cases.

## Methodology
- Narrative review / clinical guide; not a systematic review.
- Builds on foundational evidence: Araújo & Lindhe 2005 (ridge alterations), Evans & Chen 2008 (esthetic outcomes), De Rouck 2009 (immediate provisionalization), Tarnow 2003 (inter-implant bone crest), Khzam 2015 (soft-tissue alterations SR).

## Results (Take-aways)
- **Indications**: anterior single-tooth replacement, intact buccal plate, thick biotype, sufficient apical bone for primary stability, no acute infection.
- **Contraindications**: thin / fenestrated buccal wall, biotype <1 mm, acute periapical lesion, parafunction, heavy smokers, recurrent failures.
- **Surgical principles**: palatal-axis preparation, primary stability from apical + palatal bone, jumping-distance grafting, soft-tissue augmentation when biotype borderline.
- **Provisional**: ovate-pontic emergence, supports papilla, kept out of occlusion (no centric, no excursive contact).
- **Outcomes**: well-executed cases achieve esthetic outcomes acceptable to patients in ~80–90% of well-selected anterior single-tooth replacements; midfacial recession remains the main risk.
