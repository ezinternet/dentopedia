## One-line Summary

Exploratory RCT (n=20, anterior-maxilla immediate implant placement, 4-5 mo): an immediate **non-implant-supported** provisional made from the patient's own natural crown fixed to adjacent teeth with a fiber-reinforced composite (FRC) splint preserved pink esthetic score (PES 9.0 vs 6.3, intergroup z=2.799 p=0.005) and lowered early postoperative pain/swelling vs delayed restoration, with no significant difference in gingival-height change.

## 한줄요약

탐색적 RCT (전치부 상악 즉시식립, n=20, 4-5개월): 환자 자연치관을 인접치에 섬유강화복합 스플린트(FRC)로 고정하는 비-임플란트지지 즉시 임시수복이 지연수복 대비 분홍심미점수(PES 9.0 vs 6.3, z=2.799 p=0.005)를 유지하고 술후 초기 통증·종창을 줄였으며, 치은 높이 변화에는 군간 유의차가 없었다.

## Summary

This single-center exploratory RCT addresses a specific bottleneck of immediate implant placement (IIP) in the esthetic zone: conventional immediate provisionalization is implant-supported and therefore requires high primary stability, which is often unattainable when only the apical/palatal socket bone engages the implant. The authors test a non-implant-supported alternative — the patient's own extracted tooth crown, hollowed and resealed, bonded with an FRC splint to the labial surfaces of adjacent teeth chair-side — so that the provisional is stabilized by neighboring teeth rather than the implant. In 20 anterior-maxilla trauma cases (Type I sockets, intact labial wall, 4-5 month follow-up) this approach kept PES stable (whereas the delayed-restoration control dropped significantly) and produced significantly less postoperative pain and swelling on days 2-3, while gingival-height changes did not differ significantly between groups. The technique therefore offers an esthetic-preserving provisional option precisely for the cases where immediate loading is contraindicated, but the evidence is exploratory (n=20, short follow-up).

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a **fixed, non-implant-supported immediate provisional** for IIP that does not depend on primary implant stability — decoupling immediate provisionalization from its usual prerequisite.
- Re-uses the patient's **own natural crown** as the provisional, giving an exact morphology/color/emergence-profile match and chair-side delivery (no lab fabrication, unlike a Maryland bridge).
- Shows simultaneous esthetic benefit (PES preservation) and reduced early postoperative discomfort vs delayed restoration.
- Argues practical superiority over Essix/RPD (avoids transient edentulism, supports soft tissue) and over labial-vs-lingual Maryland bridges (labial FRC avoids occlusal interference).
- Uses matched pre/post intraoral optical scans for objective linear soft-tissue measurement.

## Methodology

- **Design:** exploratory RCT, CONSORT 2025, ChiCTR2300068934; two calibrated blinded examiners; operator blinded to allocation until extraction.
- **Participants (n=20):** mean 35.6 y, traumatized maxillary anterior teeth; Type I socket, intact labial wall, labial bone thickness >= 0.5 mm, adjacent teeth present, stable occlusion; 10/group by convenience (no power calculation).
- **Surgery (both arms, same surgeon):** minimally invasive extraction, micro-crestal flap, 3.3 mm bone-level tapered implant (Straumann) on palatal wall with shoulder at crest, Bio-Oss Collagen into jumping gap.
- **Experimental:** natural crown root-amputated, pulp chamber cleaned/sealed (flowable composite), trimmed to ovoid covering the site ~1-2 mm subgingivally with ~1-2 mm clearance from implant; etch-and-bond + FRC splint (RTD) on labial surfaces of crown and adjacent teeth; adjusted out of occlusion.
- **Control:** primary soft-tissue closure, delayed restoration.
- **Final restoration:** both groups at 4-5 months.
- **Outcomes:** PES (Belser, 0-10) baseline + final; gingival-height change (mesial papilla, distal papilla, mid-labial margin) by intraoral scan; pain/swelling VAS (0-10) days 1-7; implant success.
- **Statistics:** independent t-test / Mann-Whitney U (intergroup), Wilcoxon signed-rank (within-group), two-sided p<0.05.

## Results

- **Implant success:** 100% in both groups.
- **PES:** experimental stable 9.2 +- 1.1 -> 9.0 +- 0.9 (p=0.666); control fell 9.3 +- 1.3 -> 6.3 +- 1.6 (p=0.004). Baselines comparable (p=0.853); at final restoration **experimental > control, z=2.799, p=0.005.**
- **Gingival-height change (no significant intergroup difference):** mesial papilla 0.78 vs 1.05 mm (p=0.389); distal papilla 0.87 vs 1.20 mm (p=0.236); mid-labial margin 0.82 vs 0.35 mm (p=0.062).
- **Postoperative VAS (experimental lower):** day 2 pain 2.0 vs 5.1 (p=0.014), swelling 2.7 vs 5.1 (p=0.016); day 3 pain 1.1 vs 3.7 (p=0.035), swelling 1.9 vs 4.4 (p=0.039); day 7 pain p=0.049. Day 1 and days 4-6 NS.
- **Baseline labial bone (CBCT):** 1.02 +- 0.29 vs 0.9 +- 0.19 mm (p=0.269, NS).
- **Caveat:** exploratory n=20, 4-5 mo only; mid-labial margin trend (p=0.062, more change in experimental) and overall small sample mean the non-significant height results may be underpowered rather than true equivalence.
