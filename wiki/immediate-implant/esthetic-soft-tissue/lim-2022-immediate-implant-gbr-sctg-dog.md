## One-line Summary

Randomized canine study (5 dogs, 4 sites/dog: GBR / SCTG / GBR+SCTG / control, 4-month non-submerged healing, histomorphometry) — SCTG alone gave the greatest peri-implant mucosa height and thickness at/above the implant shoulder, GBR worked only below the shoulder, and combining GBR+SCTG was the *least* favorable (not additive).

## Summary

This randomized preclinical canine study supplies the histologic/histomorphometric layer that clinical immediate-implant (IIP) soft-tissue trials lack: a within-animal head-to-head of the three augmentation strategies clinicians choose between — guided bone regeneration (GBR), subepithelial connective tissue graft (SCTG), their combination (GBR/SCTG), and a no-treatment control. In 5 mongrel dogs, distal roots of maxillary P2/P3 were atraumatically extracted and narrow-diameter (Ø3.2×7 mm) implants placed immediately with a ~0.5–1.0 mm buccal gap, one of the four treatments randomly assigned per site (4 sites/dog), and 4 months of non-submerged healing.

The directional finding is a **division of labor by vertical level**: SCTG builds the *coronal* soft tissue (at and above the implant shoulder), GBR contributes only *apical* to the shoulder, and stacking the two is counterproductive — likely because the SCTG laid over the GBR membrane increases flap tension and compresses the augmented complex. All between-group differences were statistically non-significant given the small, underpowered sample, so the results are best read as mechanistic, hypothesis-generating signals that *explain* the clinical SR/MA preference for SCTG rather than as proof of superiority.

## Key Contributions

- **SCTG owns the coronal soft tissue.** Greatest peri-implant mucosa height (MM-IS median 1.99 mm) and greatest thickness at the shoulder (1.14 mm) and 1 mm above it (0.78 mm).
- **GBR is apical-only.** No mineralized tissue at or coronal to the implant shoulder in nearly all specimens; bone augmentation effective only below the shoulder.
- **Combination GBR+SCTG is not additive — it was worst.** Lowest mucosa height (MM-IS 1.24 mm) and smallest thickness at the shoulder (0.72 mm), attributed to flap tension/compression over the stacked grafts.
- Provides the **histometric mechanism** behind clinical SR/MA findings that SCTG stabilizes the mucosal margin and adds volume at IIP.

## Methodology

- **Design**: randomized controlled preclinical (animal) study; 5 mongrel dogs (>2 yr, 12–17 kg), 4 sites/dog.
- **Surgery**: maxillary P2/P3 hemi-sected, distal roots extracted atraumatically (plates preserved); immediate placement of 2-piece Ø3.2×7 mm narrow implants (Dentium NR line), shoulder at palatal crest, ~0.5–1.0 mm buccal gap; non-submerged healing.
- **Groups** (randomized, one per site): **GBR** (biphasic CaP / Osteon III in gap + 3–4 mm buccal overbuild, cross-linked collagen membrane, healing-abutment + 2 tacks); **SCTG** (palatal graft ~2× implant diameter wide × 5 mm high × ≥1.5 mm thick, sutured to palatal flap); **GBR/SCTG** (SCTG over the membrane); **Control** (none).
- **Analysis**: 4-month healing, sacrifice; non-decalcified central bucco-oral sections (40–50 μm, MMA, Masson-Goldner trichrome). Histomorphometry — vertical MM-IS, MM-fBIC; horizontal total/hard/soft tissue thickness at IS and ±1 mm. Mixed linear models with dog clustering; descriptive emphasis (sample too small for confirmatory testing).

## Results

- **Implant survival**: 2 losses (1 GBR/SCTG, 1 GBR); rest osseointegrated. All apices protruded into maxillary sinus; buccal plate moderately resorbed, bone-substitute spaces often collapsed.
- **Mucosa height (MM-IS, median)**: SCTG 1.99 > GBR 1.45 > Control 1.40 > GBR/SCTG 1.24 mm (p=0.72, NS).
- **MM-fBIC (median)**: GBR 4.06 > Control 3.97 > SCTG 3.86 > GBR/SCTG 3.06 mm (p=0.78, NS).
- **Thickness (median)** — 1 mm above IS: SCTG 0.78 (max) vs GBR/SCTG 0.30 (min); at IS: SCTG 1.14 ≈ Control 1.17 vs GBR/SCTG 0.72 (min); 1 mm below IS: SCTG 1.57 (max) vs Control 1.05 (all p>0.5, NS).
- **No mineralized tissue at/above the IS** in nearly all specimens; present 1 mm below IS in only 5 specimens (HT 0.28–1.56 mm). No between-group differences reached significance (underpowered).
