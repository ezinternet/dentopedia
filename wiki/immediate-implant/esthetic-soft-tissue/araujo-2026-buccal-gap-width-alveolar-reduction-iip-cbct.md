## One-line Summary

Retrospective CBCT cohort (n=28 maxillary central incisors, contralateral pristine-tooth control, mean 6 y in function): a DBBM-grafted buccal gap > 2 mm preserved > 90% of alveolar-process cross-sectional area (8.5% ± 11% reduction), whereas a grafted narrow gap (≤ 2 mm) lost ~41% (p<0.001); gap width was the only significant predictor of resorption, with baseline buccal bone thickness and CTG non-significant.

## 한줄요약

후향적 CBCT 코호트(상악 중절치 28부위, 반대측 자연치 대조, 평균 6년): DBBM으로 이식한 협측 gap이 2 mm 초과면 치조제 단면적의 90% 이상(8.5% 감소)을 보존했으나, 2 mm 이하 좁은 gap은 약 41% 흡수(p<0.001). gap 폭이 유일한 흡수 예측인자였고 기준 협측골 두께·결합조직이식(CTG)은 비유의.

## Summary

This is the first study to isolate **buccal-gap width** as a determinant of *dimensional ridge preservation* after immediate implant placement (Immediate Implant Placement, IIP) with deproteinized bovine bone mineral (DBBM) grafting. By restricting the sample to maxillary central incisors with an **intact buccal wall** and using the contralateral pristine tooth as a within-subject baseline, Araújo et al. cleanly separate gap width from the anatomical confounders (premolar sites, thicker walls) that muddy most IIP cohorts.

Clinical bottom line: a buccal gap **> 2 mm**, packed firmly with DBBM, behaves like a successful ridge-preservation site — preserving **> 90%** of the alveolar process. A **≤ 2 mm** gap, even when grafted, behaves like an *un-grafted* socket (~40% loss), because there is too little space to compact the biomaterial; once the thin (~0.7 mm) bundle-bone wall resorbs, the under-filled defect collapses. The healed 2-mm-thick buccal wall in the wide-gap group is **entirely newly formed bone within the grafted gap** — the original thin wall is gone regardless of treatment. This supplies the hard-tissue mechanism behind the survival benefit of a > 2 mm gap reported in the ITI Type-1A consensus.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the **> 2 mm gap → > 90% preservation** rule for the maxillary central incisor specifically (not pooled with premolars).
- Reframes the buccal wall: gap-fill volume, not baseline wall thickness, governs preservation; the original bundle bone is expendable.
- Regression isolates **gap width as the sole significant predictor**; CTG, implant design/diameter, time in function, age, and baseline buccal thickness were all non-significant.

## Methodology

- **Design**: single-practice retrospective cohort, 28 patients (one failing maxillary central incisor each), treated by one experienced periodontist (R.A.L.). 68% women, mean age 57.
- **Groups**: Wide gap > 2 mm (n=14) vs Narrow gap ≤ 2 mm (n=14); gap measured intraoperatively (UNC-15 probe + photos).
- **Protocol**: minimally invasive extraction, guided placement (Straumann/Nobel) 3 mm apical to future margin, conical healing abutment, buccal gap packed to resistance with DBBM (Bio-Oss); CTG if gingiva < 1 mm thick (wide 64.3%, narrow 57.1%). Provisional at 3 mo; final crown 2-3 mo later; SPT.
- **Imaging**: CBCT (CS 9300, voxel 0.09 mm) at mean **6 ± 4 y** (range 2-14); single blinded calibrated examiner (ICC ≥ 0.9); 3D implant template aligned to mitigate artifact.
- **Primary outcome**: % reduction in alveolar-process cross-sectional area (implant ridge vs contralateral tooth). Simple + multiple linear regression with interaction terms.

## Results

| Outcome | Wide gap (>2 mm) | Narrow gap (≤2 mm) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Total AP cross-sectional reduction (primary)** | **8.5% ± 11%** | **41.1% ± 20.2%** | **<0.001** |
| Coronal-third reduction | -28.4% | -66.8% | <0.001 |
| Middle-third reduction | -0.5% | -37.1% | <0.001 |
| Apical-third reduction | +6.9% | -17.6% | <0.001 |
| AP width reduction at 3 mm below crest | -7.4% | -52.9% | 0.001 |
| AP width reduction at 5 mm below crest | -1.2% | -42.8% | <0.001 |
| Buccal wall height reduction | -1.1% ± 9.5% | -52.3% ± 41.5% | <0.001 |
| Healed buccal bone thickness (median, implant) | 2.1 mm [1.7;2.4] | 0.5 mm [0;1] | ≤0.001 |

- **Regression**: gap > 2 vs ≤ 2 mm — adjusted beta **31.2** (95% CI 16.55-45.8, p<0.001), the only significant predictor. CTG, implant diameter/design, time in function, baseline buccal thickness all NS. GAP × function (p=0.513) and GAP × CTG (p=0.092) interactions NS. Post hoc power 99.9%.
- **Basal bone**: cross-sectional area stable; apex width more diminished in narrow group (-13.5% vs +7%, p=0.008), attributed to root/implant position rather than gap.

## Limitations

Retrospective convenience sample; single highly skilled operator (limits generalization); broad 2-14 y follow-up with evolving implant designs; CBCT artifact obscures very thin walls; contralateral-tooth design cannot fully replace true baseline 3D data; central-incisor-only, intact-buccal-wall sockets only (does not extend to dehisced or posterior sites).
