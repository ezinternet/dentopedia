## One-line Summary
Retrospective review of 42 immediate single-unit implant restorations (mean 18.9 months); buccal implant shoulder position caused ~3× greater midfacial recession than lingual (1.8 vs 0.6mm, p<0.001), making shoulder position the dominant determinant of esthetic outcome, while tissue biotype was a non-significant trend.

## Summary
Retrospective review of 42 immediate single-unit implant restorations, mean 18.9 months in function. Demonstrates that implant shoulder position (buccal vs lingual) is the dominant determinant of esthetic outcome — buccal shoulder placement results in 3× greater midfacial mucosal recession.

## Key Contributions
- Overall recession: 0.9±0.78mm (p=0.000) — statistically and clinically significant
- **Shoulder position**: buccal 1.8±0.83mm vs lingual 0.6±0.55mm recession (p=0.000) — most important factor
- Tissue biotype: thin 1.0mm vs thick 0.7mm recession — trend only (NS, p=0.187)
- No difference between implant systems (p=0.837)
- Clinical mandate: palatal implant positioning critical in anterior esthetic zone
