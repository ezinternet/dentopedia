## One-line Summary

Single case report describing a 3D-printed emergence profile former (mounted on a standard titanium healing abutment) that shapes peri-implant soft tissues and simplifies intraoral scan registration, achieving aesthetic outcome in a mandibular molar with horizontal tissue deficiency.

## Summary

This clinical report from Indiana University and Ohio State University introduces a **3D-printed emergence profile former (EPF)** as an alternative to chairside customization of healing abutments with flowable composite or acrylic. The EPF is designed in Autodesk Meshmixer (free software) using the patient's pre-operative IOS, CBCT-derived diagnostic wax-up, and a scan body as geometry references. It is fabricated in DLP autoclavable photopolymer and retained on a standard titanium stock healing abutment — no temporary abutment screw required.

In the reported case (mandibular #30, horizontal buccal bone deficiency, 1 mm attached gingiva), the EPF was placed at implant surgery and maintained through healing. At the restorative appointment it served dual purpose: soft tissue conditioner and IOS registration aid. Post-healing buccal volume enhancement was observed, and an aesthetically pleasing crown was delivered without buccal bone augmentation.

## Key Contributions

- **Novel retention mechanism**: EPF sleeve retains on the titanium stock healing abutment's collar geometry (measured with Boley gauge), eliminating the need for a separate temporary abutment screw or custom abutment
- **Free-software workflow**: Autodesk Meshmixer 3.5 used for Boolean subtraction of the offset scan body from the extended wax-up geometry
- **Dual function**: simultaneous soft-tissue conditioner and scan body surrogate for IOS registration
- **Reduced chairside time** vs. conventional flowable composite customization

## Methodology

**Planning**: CoDiagnostiX 10.8.1; Straumann BLX Ø5.0×10 mm with slight lingual inclination to avoid buccal bone augmentation. Static CAIS surgical template (DLP, Straumann P Pro resin).

**EPF design sequence**:
1. Measure healing abutment (Boley gauge): gingival Ø, platform Ø, gingival height
2. Import preop IOS + scan body + implant analog into Meshmixer
3. Modify scan body dimensions to match healing abutment; close screw access
4. Import diagnostic wax-up; extend apically; sculpt gingival contours
5. Boolean subtract offset scan body (0.02 mm) → internal sleeve
6. Horizontal cut at greatest buccal height of contour → final STL
7. Print in autoclavable DLP photopolymer

**Clinical sequence**: implant placement → EPF seated → healing phase → IOS with EPF → EPF removed → finalize prosthetics.

## Results

- Enhanced buccal soft tissue volume post-healing despite pre-existing horizontal deficiency
- Aesthetic emergence profile without buccal grafting
- Simplified IOS: peri-implant contours and implant position registered simultaneously
- No adverse tissue reactions reported
