CBCT cross-sectional study (n=90 Mongoloid subjects) reporting incisive canal mean length 16.33 mm and width 3.85 mm, with males having significantly larger canals and thicker anterior maxillary bone, and anterior bone thinning with age even in dentate patients.

## Summary

This retrospective CBCT morphometric study from the University of Malaya measured incisive canal (IC), incisive foramen (IF), nasal foramen (NF), and anterior maxillary bone thickness in 90 Mongoloid (Chinese and Malay) subjects aged 15–75. Key anatomical findings directly relevant to immediate implant placement in the anterior maxilla:

- IC mean length: 16.33 mm; mid-canal width: 3.85 mm
- IF: 2.80 mm (labiopalatal) × 3.49 mm (mesiodistal)
- NF: 6.06 mm labiopalatal
- Anterior maxillary bone thickness: 7.63 mm (mean) — the labial-to-IC safety margin for palatally angulated implants
- Canal predominantly funnel-shaped (wider at nasal end), slanted, and curved
- Gender dimorphism: males show significantly larger IC width, IF, and anterior bone thickness
- Age effect: anterior bone thins with age (p < 0.05) but IC dimensions remain stable

The Mongoloid population in this study showed longer, more slanted-curved IC with narrower IF compared to European reports, implying that standard Caucasian-derived planning norms may underestimate palatally-angled IC encroachment risk in Asian patients.

## Key Contributions

1. **Ethnic normative data**: First CBCT normative dataset for Mongoloid (Malaysian Chinese and Malay) IC/IF dimensions — fills a gap vs Western-derived norms.
2. **Gender dimorphism**: Males have larger IC, larger IF, and thicker anterior bone; females are at higher relative encroachment risk per unit of palatal angulation.
3. **Age-bone interaction**: Anterior maxillary bone thins with age even in fully dentate subjects — older patients require more conservative implant angulation regardless of canal size.
4. **Canal morphology**: Funnel shape + slanted-curved course means the IC occupies more palatal volume at mid-implant depth; important for assessing gap-to-IC relationships in immediate placement.
5. **Safety margin reference**: Mean 7.63 mm anterior bone provides a benchmark — implants angulated palatally to avoid buccal concavity must not exceed this threshold.

## Methodology

- **Design**: Retrospective CBCT image review (cross-sectional)
- **n**: 90 (46 M / 44 F; ages 15–75; Chinese and Malay ethnic groups)
- **Equipment**: i-CAT CBCT (120 kVp, 3–7 mA, 20 s, 0.3 mm voxel); Simplant v13
- **Measurements**: IF LP/MD diameter, NF LP diameter, IC length and mid-width, anterior maxillary bone thickness (3-level average), IC channel count, course, direction
- **Statistics**: t-test / Mann-Whitney U, ANOVA / Kruskal-Wallis, chi-square; p < 0.05
- **Reliability**: ICC 0.97 (quantitative), κ 0.92 (categorical)

## Results

| Measurement | Mean ± SD | Gender effect |
|---|---|---|
| IF labiopalatal | 2.80 mm | M > F* |
| IF mesiodistal | 3.49 mm | M > F* |
| NF labiopalatal | 6.06 mm | NS |
| IC length | 16.33 mm | NS |
| IC mid-width | 3.85 mm | M > F* |
| Anterior bone thickness | 7.63 mm | M > F* |

*p < 0.05

- Canal course: predominantly slanted + curved (vs straight-vertical in some European series)
- Mid-canal channels: single channel majority; bifurcated minority
- Anterior bone thickness decreases with age (significant); IC dimensions do not
- Bone shape labial to IC: mostly straight or concave — concave profile further reduces effective implant-to-IC clearance
