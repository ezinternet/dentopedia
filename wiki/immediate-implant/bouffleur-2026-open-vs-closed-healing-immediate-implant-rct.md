## 한줄요약
Heidelberg Univ RCT (Int J Implant Dent 2026, n=46/48 implants): 즉시 임플란트 open vs closed healing — Pink Esthetic Score (PES) open group이 closed보다 유의하게 우수 (MD -1.49, 95% CI -2.36 to -0.62, p=0.0014). 12개월 결과.

## Summary
Prospective RCT comparing open vs closed (submerged) healing approaches in immediate implant placement. Forty-six patients received a total of 48 implants, randomized to open healing (n=25) or closed healing (n=23). Clinical and radiographic assessment at three timepoints: T1 pre-extraction, T2 3 months post-op, T3 12 months post-op.

Primary endpoint Pink Esthetic Score (PES; 7 soft tissue parameters, max 14): open healing group showed significantly higher PES outcomes (mean difference -1.49 favoring open, 95% CI -2.36 to -0.62, p=0.0014). A higher baseline (specific parameter in paper) also predicted better outcomes. Secondary outcomes included implant survival and volumetric tissue changes.

## Key Contributions
- Direct RCT comparison of open vs closed healing in immediate implant placement — long-debated technique choice.
- PES superiority of open healing quantified.
- Single linear mixed-effects model for repeated-measures analysis.

## Methodology
Prospective RCT; randomized 1:1; 3 assessment timepoints; primary PES; secondary implant survival + volumetric change; linear mixed-effects model.

## Limitations
- Single-center (Heidelberg)
- 12-month follow-up (no longer-term aesthetic data)
- Patient esthetic preference / pre-op tissue biotype variability

## Clinical Implication
For Korean immediate implant aesthetic-zone cases: open healing protocol is supported as the PES-superior choice when wound closure permits. For cases with thin biotype, gap distance, or expected significant volumetric resorption, closed healing may still be defensible for biologic reasons not addressed by PES alone.
