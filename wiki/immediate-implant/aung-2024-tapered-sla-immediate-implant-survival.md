## One-line Summary

Retrospective single-surgeon study of 49 immediately placed tapered SLA internal-submerged bone-level implants (34 patients, mean 7.43 yr follow-up): cumulative 5-yr survival 93.88% — 100% in the mandible and both premolar regions, lowest in maxillary anterior (85.38%) — with all 3 failures maxillary sinus–related and progressive marginal bone gain over 5 years.

## Summary

Aung et al. (2024) retrospectively followed 49 dental implants immediately placed after extraction in 34 patients at Seoul National University Dental Hospital (2009–2018), all by a single surgeon, using tapered, sand-blasted and acid-etched (SLA) internal submerged **bone-level** implants (Luna®, Shinhung). Mean patient age was 65.91 yr and mean follow-up 7.43 yr (range 5–14 yr). The headline is a **positional rationale**: survival of immediate placement depends heavily on jaw region. Cumulative 5-yr survival was **93.88%**, with **100% across the entire mandible and in both premolar regions**, but only **85.38% in the maxillary anterior** and 95.83% in the maxillary molar. All three failures (6.12%, in 2 patients) were in the maxilla and **associated with maxillary sinus pathology** (sinusitis, pneumatization, prior sinus surgery). Rather than the bone loss typical of socket healing, the SLA surfaces showed **progressive marginal bone gain** from 3 months through 5 years (mesial −0.68 ± 1.61 mm at 3 mo; p<0.05 vs baseline). The clinical message: immediate placement is dependable in the mandible and premolar sites, demands caution and case selection in the maxillary anterior/molar, and should defer to delayed placement with sinus lift/graft when maxillary sinus pathology is present.

## Key Contributions

- Region-by-region 5-yr survival map for immediately placed tapered SLA **bone-level** implants, converting outcomes into site-specific decision logic.
- Identifies **maxillary sinus pathology** as the unifying cause of all failures — the actionable risk flag for maxillary immediate placement.
- Demonstrates **marginal bone gain (not loss)** around immediate SLA implants over 5 years, with 91.8% of cases placed without additional bone graft.
- Provides a Korean (Shinhung Luna®) implant-system long-term dataset to pair with the pooled estimates in immediate-vs-delayed meta-analyses.

## Methodology

- Retrospective cohort, single surgeon, single center (SNUDH); IRB S-D20200007.
- **49 implants / 34 patients**, 2009–2018; inclusion required immediate placement and ≥5 yr panoramic follow-up.
- Flapless atraumatic extraction preferred; multi-rooted teeth sectioned to preserve interradicular septum and buccal plate; anterior fixtures placed slightly palatal (≥1.5 mm buccal wall), posterior in mid-septum; immediate implants seated deeper than healed-site implants; two-stage (re-entry 3–6 mo, prosthesis 2–3 mo later).
- Survival graded on the ICOI Pisa Consensus scale (survival = 2 mm–half-body bone loss, no mobility).
- MBL measured on distortion-corrected panoramic radiographs at T0/3 mo/3 mo & 1 yr post-loading/5 yr.
- SPSS 26; one-way ANOVA for survival by location/diameter/length; independent-sample test and Shapiro–Wilk for MBL (p<0.05).

## Results

- **Distribution**: 23 mandible / 26 maxilla; 13 anterior, 12 premolar, 24 (48.98%) molar. Common sizes: 4 mm diameter (40.82%), 8.5 mm length (53.36%).
- **Cumulative 5-yr survival: 93.88%.**
  - Mandible (anterior, premolar, molar): **100%**.
  - Maxilla premolar: **100%**; maxillary molar: 95.83%; **maxillary anterior: 85.38%** (lowest).
- **By diameter**: 3.5 & 5 mm = 100%; 4 mm = 96.2%; 4.5 mm = 84.61% (not significant).
- **By length**: 10 & 11.5 mm = 100%; 7 mm = 87.5%; 8.5 mm = 93.75% (not significant).
- **MBL**: significant marginal bone **gain** from 3 months (mesial −0.68 ± 1.61 mm), progressive and significant mesial+distal through 5 years vs baseline; consecutive-visit changes not significant.
- **Failures**: 3/49 (6.12%), all maxillary and **sinus-related** — #16 (HTN/renal transplant; sinusitis → osseointegration failure at 5-mo re-entry) and #13/#23 (denture patient, bilateral sinus pneumatization; peri-implantitis pre-loading). All successfully replaced after sinus lift/graft.
- **Comorbidity**: 61.74% metabolic and 8.82% bone disease, without a clear independent association with failure.
