## Summary
Narrative literature review (Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, JCM 2025) comparing immediate, early, and delayed dental implant placement timing, and evaluating loading protocols. No single superior timing exists — patient selection criteria determine outcomes, particularly in esthetic zones.

## Key Contributions
- **Immediate implant placement**: survival 93.8–100%; increased risk of gingival recession and implant exposure
- **Early placement** (4–8 wk): good outcomes; reduced esthetic risk vs immediate
- **Delayed placement** (>6 mo): most predictable; lowest esthetic risk
- **Immediate loading**: viable when primary stability criteria met; not universally applicable
- Variables analyzed: survival rate, success rate, complications, patient-reported outcomes
- 9774 articles identified; RCTs, prospective cohorts, and retrospective studies included

## Timing Comparison
| Timing | Survival | Esthetic Risk | Indication |
|--------|---------|---------------|------------|
| Immediate (Type 1) | 93.8–100% | Highest | Intact wall, thick biotype, no infection |
| Early 4–8 wk (Type 2) | High | Moderate | Soft tissue healing desired |
| Early 12–16 wk (Type 3) | High | Low-moderate | Partial bone fill desired |
| Delayed >6 mo (Type 4) | >95% | Lowest | Complex cases, augmentation needed |
