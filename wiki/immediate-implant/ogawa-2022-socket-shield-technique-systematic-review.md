## Summary
Systematic review (Japan Prosthodontic Society) of 20 studies covering 288 patients treated with the socket shield technique (SST) combined with immediate implant placement, follow-up 3–60 months. Demonstrates feasibility with 9.5% complication rate, but evidence base is predominantly case reports with low methodological quality.

## Key Contributions
- 20 studies: 1 RCT, 2 cohort, 14 case reports, 3 retrospective case series
- 288 patients; follow-up range 3–60 months
- Complication/adverse event rate: **9.5%** (26/274 cases)
- Survival without complications: **90.5%**
- Quality assessment: 12/20 studies rated good quality
- Cases >12 months: mostly good esthetic appearance documented
- Long-term predictability: cannot be confirmed — insufficient high-quality evidence

## Complication Types
| Complication | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Failed osseointegration | Implant loss |
| Shield mobility + infection | Shield movement, secondary infection |
| Shield exposure | Shield becomes visible through mucosa |
| Shield migration | Displacement of retained root fragment |
| Apical root resorption | Resorption of retained root |

## Socket Shield Technique Principle
- Retain a thin buccal root fragment in the socket
- Preserves bundle bone (PDL blood supply maintained)
- Immediate implant placed palatal to shield
- Aims to prevent buccal bone collapse without grafting
