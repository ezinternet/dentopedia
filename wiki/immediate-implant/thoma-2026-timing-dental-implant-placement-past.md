## Summary
Perspective article from University of Zurich (Thoma, Jung) reviewing the ITI Type 1–4 implant timing classification, evidence-based criteria for each protocol, clinical gray zones, and the future shift toward AI-guided personalized timing. Core message: **"Time truly is everything"** — the art is knowing when to accelerate and when to wait.

## ITI Timing Classification

| Type                      | Timing            | Survival | When to Use                                                  |
| ------------------------- | ----------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **1 (Immediate)**         | Day of extraction | 95–98%   | Intact socket, no infection, IT ≥35 Ncm                      |
| **2 (Early/soft tissue)** | 4–8 weeks         | 96–99%   | Minor infection, thin buccal plate, GBR planned              |
| **3 (Early/bone)**        | 12–16 weeks       | 97–99%   | Periapical pathology, suboptimal socket                      |
| **4 (Delayed)**           | >6 months         | >95%     | Extensive bone loss, major augmentation, low esthetic demand |

## Type 1 (Immediate) Prerequisites
- No acute infection / no active periodontitis
- Intact 4-wall socket
- **IT ≥ 35 Ncm** (primary stability confirmed)
- Gap grafting between implant and socket wall
- ⚠️ Thin periodontal phenotype + high smile line → higher midfacial recession risk

## Key Clinical Takeaways
- **Type 2 is often safer than Type 1** for thin phenotype patients — 4 weeks delays costs minimal esthetic compromise for major soft tissue benefit
- **Type 1 in non-intact sockets**: limited dehiscences do not automatically rule out immediate placement if primary stability achievable + regeneration planned
- **Endodontically treated teeth with periapical lesion**: borderline for Type 1; residual pathology debate unresolved
- **Combined risk factors** (smoking + thin phenotype + periodontitis history): unpredictable combined effect — lean toward later timing
- **Osseointegration ≠ esthetic success**: biological integration can occur while midfacial recession develops over years

## The Future Principle
> *"When for this patient, in this socket, under these systemic and local conditions"*  
> — not "wait 3–6 months"

Emerging tools:
- **AI + CBCT**: risk stratification for timing decisions (bone volume, density, phenotype, systemic factors)
- **Bioactive materials**: accelerated socket healing → earlier safe placement
- **Soft tissue augmentation**: CTG, volume-stable collagen matrices → reduce Type 1 esthetic risk
