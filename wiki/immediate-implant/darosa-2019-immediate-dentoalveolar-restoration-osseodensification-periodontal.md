## One-line Summary
Two-case clinical report combining the Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration (IDR) technique (autogenous tuberosity bone block reconstruction of the buccal plate) with osseodensification (OD) osteotomy in periodontally compromised extraction sockets: OD qualitatively raised insertion torque enough to support immediate provisionalization. Proof-of-concept only (n=2, no comparator, paywalled — no extractable quantitative outcomes); do not generalize.

## Summary
This is a two-case clinical report illustrating a workflow that combines the Immediate Dentoalveolar Restoration (IDR) technique — autogenous tuberosity bone block reconstruction of the buccal plate at the time of extraction — with osseodensification (OD) osteotomy preparation, applied to periodontally compromised extraction sockets where conventional immediate implant placement would struggle to obtain primary stability.

Two cases of severe alveolar bone loss were treated. Both received atraumatic extraction, IDR reconstruction of the missing buccal plate using autogenous tuberosity bone, OD osteotomy with Densah burs, and immediate implant placement. Authors qualitatively report that OD raised insertion torque sufficient for immediate provisionalization and that soft- and hard-tissue positioning remained favorable at follow-up assessed by clinical exam, photography, radiography, and CBCT. The paper positions OD as a primary-stability enhancer that makes IDR more predictable in compromised sites.

## Key Contributions
- Combined IDR + OD protocol for immediate implant placement in periodontally compromised sockets.
- Qualitative evidence that OD raises insertion torque enough to support immediate provisionalization in compromised sites.
- CBCT-based hard-tissue assessment of soft- and hard-tissue stability at follow-up.
- Proof-of-concept; not a controlled trial.

## Methodology
Two consecutive clinical cases. Workflow: (1) atraumatic extraction of periodontally hopeless tooth; (2) IDR — autogenous bone block from maxillary tuberosity shaped to reconstruct buccal-plate defect; (3) OD osteotomy preparation with Densah burs in counterclockwise mode (CCW) into the host bone/graft; (4) immediate implant placement with provisional restoration; (5) follow-up by clinical exam, photographs, radiographs, and CBCT. No randomization, no comparator group, no formal statistical analysis. Outcomes are descriptive.

## Results
Numerical detail not extractable from available text — abstract-level only (publisher paywall). Authors describe:
- Higher insertion torque after OD osteotomy versus what would be expected with conventional drilling in these compromised sites (no Ncm value reported in extractable abstract).
- Both cases proceeded to immediate provisionalization.
- Soft- and hard-tissue positioning maintained on radiographic/CBCT follow-up.

## Limitations
- n = 2; this is the weakest evidence tier (case report) and supports only hypothesis generation.
- Publisher paywall — no extractable numerical outcomes (Ncm, ISQ, MBL); claims rely on authors' narrative.
- No comparator (IDR alone, or OD alone, or conventional drilling).
- One author (Huwais) is the inventor of the OD technique — disclose-and-interpret-with-caution.
- "Improved primary stability" in the abstract is not backed by extractable quantitative measurement in the available text — flagged per living-document principle.
