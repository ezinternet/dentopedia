## Summary

This cross-sectional survey of 186 Lithuanian oral surgeons and periodontists examined the factors driving the decision to place immediate implants. A periapical lesion exceeding 5 mm was the dominant contraindication for both groups, and esthetics with anatomy preservation was the chief perceived advantage. Decision criteria diverged by specialty — periodontists weighed socket soft-tissue quality, oral surgeons weighed bone-wall morphology — and only about a third to two-fifths routinely used established extraction-socket morphology classifications, revealing an evidence-to-practice gap the authors address with an education recommendation.

## Key Contributions

- Documents real-world immediate-implant decision criteria across two specialties.
- Highlights low uptake of socket-morphology classification systems.

## Methodology

- Anonymous online questionnaire (Jan–Feb 2024); 186 specialists; chi-square analysis.

## Results

- Periapical lesion >5 mm contraindication: 91.7% (OS) / 96.9% (perio).
- Esthetics/preservation advantage: 99.2% (OS) / 92.3% (perio).
- Socket classification use: perio 43.1%, OS 33.9%.
