## One-line Summary

Network meta-analysis of 17 RCTs comparing denture adhesive types finds cream and powder adhesives most effective for mandibular complete denture retention (Kapur's index) and premolar/molar bite force (moderate certainty evidence).

## Summary

This 2024 systematic review with network meta-analysis (NMA) is the first to directly compare different types of denture adhesives (cream, powder, strip, cushion) in complete denture wearers using SUCRA rankings. Searching Scopus, PubMed, and Cochrane CENTRAL from inception to July 2023, 17 RCTs were included in the quantitative synthesis (PROSPERO: CRD42023451045). The NMA found cream adhesives significantly improved bite force (incisal: RR=7.63; premolar/molar: RR=33.66) and denture retention via multiple measures (Kapur's index maxillary RR=0.80; mandibular RR=1.27; reduced dislodgments RR=-5.35). Powder adhesives significantly increased premolar/molar bite force (RR=36.00) and mandibular Kapur's index retention (RR=1.54). Strip and cushion adhesives did not achieve statistical significance for any primary outcome. GRADE assessment rated certainty as moderate for the significant findings. The clinical recommendation is that cream and powder adhesives are effective first-line options for improving complete denture function and retention.

## Key Contributions

- First NMA directly comparing adhesive types (cream vs powder vs strip vs cushion) in complete denture wearers
- SUCRA-based ranking provides clinically actionable hierarchy of adhesive types
- Confirms cream adhesives as versatile performers (effective for both bite force and retention)
- Identifies powder adhesives as superior for mandibular Kapur's index retention
- Demonstrates strip and cushion adhesives lack significant evidence for any tested outcome
- Moderate GRADE certainty supports clinical use of cream and powder adhesives
- Extends and refines Shu et al. (2021) which showed general DA efficacy without type-level comparison

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review + Network Meta-Analysis (NMA)
- **Databases**: Scopus, PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL (inception to July 2023)
- **PROSPERO**: CRD42023451045
- **Studies included**: 17 RCTs in quantitative analysis
- **Adhesive types**: Cream, powder, strip, cushion
- **Key outcomes**: Bite force (incisal; premolar/molar), denture retention (Kapur's index maxillary/mandibular, dislodgment count), masticatory performance, OHRQoL
- **Ranking method**: SUCRA (Surface Under the Cumulative Ranking)
- **Evidence quality**: GRADE framework

## Results

| Outcome | Adhesive | Result | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bite force — incisal | Cream | RR = 7.63 (95% CI: 3.34, 11.91) | < .05 |
| Bite force — premolar/molar | Cream | RR = 33.66 (95% CI: 15.15, 52.16) | < .05 |
| Bite force — premolar/molar | Powder | RR = 36.00 (95% CI: 16.20, 55.79) | < .05 |
| Kapur's index — maxillary | Cream | RR = 0.80 (95% CI: 0.18, 1.42) | 0.012 |
| Kapur's index — mandibular | Cream | RR = 1.27 (95% CI: 0.67, 1.87) | < .05 |
| Kapur's index — mandibular | Powder | RR = 1.54 (95% CI: 0.58, 2.49) | 0.002 |
| Denture dislodgments | Cream | RR = -5.35 (95% CI: -9.93, -0.77) | 0.022 |

**Key negative findings**: Strip and cushion adhesives showed no statistically significant benefit for any primary outcome. No adhesive type achieved significance for masticatory performance or OHRQoL measures.

**SUCRA Rankings**:
- Mandibular retention (Kapur's): Powder > Cream > Strip/Cushion
- Bite force (incisal): Cream > others
- Bite force (premolar/molar): Powder and Cream comparably ranked

**GRADE certainty**: Moderate for cream and powder adhesive findings on retention and bite force
