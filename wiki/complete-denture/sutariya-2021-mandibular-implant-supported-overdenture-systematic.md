## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 6 RCTs (2010–2020) on mandibular 2-implant overdenture attachments: ball beats locator in prosthodontic complications (RR 0.55, p=0.03); bar ≈ telescopic in peri-implant outcomes; bar = best retention; telescopic = best patient satisfaction and mucosal health.

## Summary

This PRISMA-compliant SR+MA from the Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society (2021) compared four mandibular implant overdenture attachment systems — ball/stud, bar and clip, magnet/locator, and telescopic — across 6 RCTs/crossover trials published 2010–2020. Only studies with ≥2 interforaminal implants, delayed loading, and ≥1 year follow-up were included.

Out of four pre-specified outcomes (prosthodontic complications, retention, peri-implant tissue changes, patient satisfaction), meta-analysis was feasible for only two due to limited data:

1. **Ball vs. Locator (prosthodontic complications)**: Ball attachment significantly fewer complications (RR = 0.55, 95% CI, p = 0.03; n=48, single RCT)
2. **Milled bar vs. Telescopic (peri-implant tissue)**: No significant difference in probing depth (RR = 0.20, p = 0.74) or marginal bone loss (MD = 0.35 mm, p = 0.10; n=45, single RCT)

Retention and patient satisfaction were reviewed qualitatively: bar attachment provided superior retention; telescopic attachment showed highest patient satisfaction and least peri-implant mucosal changes.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA to specifically cover the 2010–2020 literature on mandibular overdenture attachment systems using PRISMA methodology
- Provides comparative framework across 4 attachment types on 4 clinically relevant outcomes
- Identifies telescopic attachment as underutilized option with favorable mucosal and satisfaction outcomes
- Confirms ball attachment as practical choice for limited inter-arch space and non-parallel implants
- Highlights evidence gap: insufficient pooled data for retention and satisfaction meta-analysis

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA, PRISMA guidelines
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane CENTRAL, ScienceDirect + manual hand search
- **Inclusion**: RCTs/crossover trials, 2010–2020, mandibular arch, ≥2 interforaminal implants, delayed loading, ≥1 year follow-up
- **Exclusion**: maxillary overdentures, single-implant overdentures, immediate loading, non-RCT designs
- **Screening**: 235 identified → 122 full-text → 6 included
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane tool; all 6 studies = low risk
- **Statistics**: RevMan 5.4; RR for dichotomous data, MD for continuous data, 95% CI

## Results

### Meta-analysis

| Comparison | Outcome | Result | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ball vs. Locator | Prosthodontic complications | RR = 0.55 (ball favored) | 0.03 |
| Bar vs. Telescopic | Probing depth | RR = 0.20 (NS) | 0.74 |
| Bar vs. Telescopic | Marginal bone loss | MD = 0.35 mm (NS) | 0.10 |

### Qualitative synthesis

| Attachment | Retention | Patient Satisfaction | Peri-implant Tissue | Complication burden |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ball/stud | Good | Good | Moderate | Low (vs. locator) |
| Bar | Best | High | Moderate | Moderate (technical) |
| Magnet/Locator | Lowest | Lower | Variable | Higher (demagnetization, wear) |
| Telescopic | Good | Best | Best | Low |

### Clinical decision guide

- **Limited inter-arch space or non-parallel implants** → Ball/stud
- **Maximum retention priority** → Bar
- **Best peri-implant mucosal health + patient satisfaction** → Telescopic
- **Cost/simplicity** → Ball/stud
- **Splinting benefit needed** → Bar
