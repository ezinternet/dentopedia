## One-line Summary

Cross-sectional clinical comparison (n=34 dentate, stable MIP) using a KaVo Arcus Digma 2 ultrasound mandibular motion analyser (50 µm accuracy) ranking 7 centric-relation determination techniques: Dawson bimanual manipulation and tongue-tip at the hard/soft-palate border deviated least from the MIP reference (no significant deviation on any of the 3 axes), while active/passive chin retrusion seated the condyle posteriorly and the gothic-arch tracer captured it anteriorly — all deviations within ±1 mm.

## 한줄요약

단면 임상 비교(안정 최대교두감합위(MIP)를 가진 유치악 34명), KaVo Arcus Digma 2 초음파 하악운동분석기(정확도 50 µm)로 중심위(Centric Relation, CR) 결정법 7가지를 순위화: Dawson 양수조작법과 혀끝을 경구개·연구개 경계에 두는 방법이 MIP 기준에서 가장 적게 벗어났고(세 축 모두 유의차 없음), 능동·수동 턱 후퇴는 과두를 후방으로, 고딕아치 트레이서는 전방으로 이동시켰다 — 모든 편차는 ±1 mm 이내.

## Summary

Centric relation (CR) registration is the anteroposterior/superior half of jaw-relation determination for full-mouth rehabilitation, complete dentures, and orthognathic planning (the vertical half being OVD). This study asks a practical question: of the manual and intraoral CR techniques dentists actually use, which one captures the condyle closest to a reproducible reference, and which ones risk seating it in a forced posterior (retruded) position? Rather than the traditional articulator-mounting (mm scale, mounting error) or CBCT/MRI (cost, radiation), it uses a **KaVo Arcus Digma 2 ultrasound mandibular motion analyser** (claimed 50 µm accuracy) to read each technique's resulting condylar position in a 3D coordinate system zeroed to each subject's **maximum intercuspal position (MIP)** — chosen as the most reproducible practical reference (the study is explicit this is a relative benchmark, not an anatomic CR gold standard).

**Bottom line**: in dentate patients with a stable MIP, **Dawson's bimanual manipulation** gave the smallest deviation (no significant difference from MIP on any axis), with **tongue-tip at the hard/soft-palate border** (linguomandibular homotrophy) second — also non-significant on all axes. This concordance with a 2021 systematic review (which found Dawson most accurate in dentate patients) is the page's main reinforcing finding.

## Key Contributions

- **Largest single-cohort head-to-head of CR techniques** — seven everyday methods (A gothic arch tracer, B adduction field, C Dawson bimanual, D tongue at palatal rugae, E tongue at hard/soft-palate border, F active chin retrusion, G passive 20 N chin push) against one digital reference.
- **Direction-of-error framing, not just magnitude** — the clinically useful split is *which way* a method misses MIP: anterior (gothic-arch family, tongue-at-rugae) vs posterior (both retrusion methods) vs near-zero (Dawson, hard/soft-palate tongue).
- **Reassurance on magnitude** — even the 5 significantly-deviating methods stayed within **±1 mm**, so non-retrusive methods remain clinically acceptable.

## Methodology

- **Design / setting**: cross-sectional, single-examiner (20 y gnathology experience), Semmelweis University, 2014–2018. Ethics No. 92/2013.
- **Sample**: 34 volunteers (24 F / 10 M), mean 29.1 ± 7.3 y; dentate, **stable MIP, TMD-negative** (DC-TMD screened), no bruxism/parafunction, no prior orthodontics. Edentulous excluded.
- **Reference**: KaVo Arcus Digma 2 — 4 mandibular ultrasonic transmitters + 8 maxillary receivers; paraocclusal clutch; facebow to Frankfort horizontal plane; EPA module calibrated to MIP. Origin = MIP; x = sagittal (+ant/−post), y = vertical (+cranial/−caudal), z = transverse.
- **Reliability**: intraexaminer weighted kappa 0.88–0.93.
- **Stats**: mean + 95% CI per axis (α 0.05); significant if CI excludes 0. GraphPad Prism.
- **Note**: gothic-arch (A) and adduction-field (B) were registered at a slightly elevated OVD (the intraoral tracer requires an open position) — a confounder for their anterior-inferior deviation.

## Results

**Which method best matched the digital motion-analyser reference (MIP)?**

| Method | Sagittal R / L (mm) | Vertical R / L (mm) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C — Dawson bimanual** | 0 / 0.01 (ns) | 0.17 / 0.18 (ns) | **Best — no sig. deviation any axis** |
| **E — Tongue at hard/soft-palate border** | 0.10 / 0.13 (ns) | 0.23 / 0.18 (ns) | **2nd — no sig. deviation any axis** |
| A — Gothic-arch apex | 0.28* / 0.30* | −0.5* / −0.47* | anterior + downward |
| B — Adduction field | 0.37* / 0.29 | −0.47* / −0.38 | anterior + downward (side-variable) |
| D — Tongue at palatal rugae | 0.78* / 0.79* | −0.24 / −0.29* | **largest sagittal** — too protrusive |
| F — Active retrusion | −0.24* / −0.28* | 0.34* / 0.34* | **posterior** seat |
| G — Passive 20 N chin push | −0.35* / −0.46* | −0.05 / −0.06 (ns) | **posterior** seat |

(*= significantly different from MIP, 95% CI excludes 0; ns = not significant. Transverse z-axis negligible for all, 0 mm [−0.03, 0.03].)

- **Coincided with MIP on all three axes**: **Dawson (C)** and **tongue at hard/soft-palate border (E)**.
- **5 of 7 methods** significantly deviated on ≥1 axis — but all within **±1 mm**.
- **Posterior (caution)**: active retrusion (F) and passive 20 N chin push (G) seated the condyle posteriorly; highly reproducible per literature but risk a forced retruded position.
- **Anterior**: tongue-at-rugae (D) most protrusive; gothic-arch apex (A) and adduction field (B) anterior-and-downward (condylar translation at the open registration).
- **Clinical recommendation**: methods that do **not** cause posterior deviation (A, B, C, D, E) are clinically applicable; retrusion methods (F, G) are the ones to avoid for capturing a physiologic seat.
