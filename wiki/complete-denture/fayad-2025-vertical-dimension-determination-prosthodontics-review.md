Comprehensive narrative review (79 studies, Cureus 2025) cataloging OVD-determination methods from classical (facial/anthropometric, phonetics, swallowing, physiologic rest + freeway space, cephalometry) to digital (3D facial scan, CBCT, CAD/CAM, AI); no single method is universally superior, so a combined traditional + digital multifactorial approach is recommended.

## Summary

Part II of a two-part Cureus series, this review screened 1,259 records (903 after de-dup) and included 79 studies on how to determine the **occlusal vertical dimension (OVD / VDO)** in prosthodontics — the facial height (nose-tip to chin) with teeth in maximal intercuspation. The challenge is acute in **complete-denture / edentulous** patients, where natural reference points are gone. The review traces the field from Leonardo's facial ratios and Niswonger's physiologic rest position through phonetics and cephalometry to modern digital and AI workflows, and — most relevant clinically — describes how an **incorrect VD shows on the face**.

## Key Contributions

**Two-stream taxonomy of OVD determination:**

- **Pre-extraction record methods** — transfer the natural-dentition OVD via intra-oral measurement, profile tracing, cephalometry, or the patient's existing denture.
- **Post-extraction assessment methods:**
  - *Physiological/functional* — physiologic rest position minus **freeway space** (~2–4 mm); swallowing technique; **phonetic** methods (sibilant "s"/"ch"; closest speaking space ~1–2 mm; Igić vowel "O" ≈ 5.5 mm / "E" ≈ 7.5 mm); neuromuscular/tactile (Lytle central bearing device); mastication-based.
  - *Mechanical* — paralleling the ridges (Wright); existing complete denture (Massad).
  - *Measurement devices* — Willis gauge (subnasion→gnathion), calipers/dividers, Knebelman craniometer, Boos bimeter, Precise Jaw Relation Recorder; electronic: EMG + jaw trackers, TENS, K7 kinesiography.
  - *Radiographic/imaging* — cephalometry, digital cephalometry, a panoramic **inter-mental-foramina** OVD formula (Fayad et al.), 3D facial scanning.
  - *Esthetic* — facial-thirds proportion, lip support, lip–teeth relationship, digital smile design.
  - *Digital/AI* — virtual articulators, intraoral-scanner virtual occlusal records, AI for prosthesis design and outcome prediction.

## Methodology

Narrative/comprehensive literature review (PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Web of Science, Cochrane). No meta-analysis or pooled estimates; methods are organized functionally and appraised qualitatively. Evidence weight is therefore that of an expert narrative synthesis.

## Results

- **No universally superior method.** Traditional techniques are accessible but lack reproducibility and are population-dependent; digital methods (3D scan, cephalometry, CAD) add precision and documentation at the cost of equipment and training. Best practice = **combine techniques + digital tools**, individualized.
- **Key reference numbers:** freeway space ≈ **2–4 mm** (RVD − OVD); closest speaking space ≈ **1–2 mm** anterior clearance on sibilants.
- **Signs of EXCESSIVE (increased) VD:** muscle fatigue, speech difficulty, clicking of teeth during speech, accelerated **bone/ridge resorption**, prosthetic instability, trauma to supporting tissues, and a stretched/strained appearance with the lower facial third too long.
- **Signs of REDUCED (decreased) VD:** impaired mastication, TMD, patient dissatisfaction, and an **aged/overclosed face** — shortened lower facial third, **deepened mentolabial (labiomental) fold**, drooping commissures, increased **mentalis strain / chin prominence**, and loss of lip support. *(This is the user's clinical chairside read of overclosure.)*
- **Method caveats:** Willis-gauge assumption of equal upper/lower facial thirds fails in some populations (Filipino sample: upper exceeded lower by ~3 cm); Boos bimeter tends to **overestimate** VD; calipers/Willis confounded by soft-tissue compression at skin markers.
