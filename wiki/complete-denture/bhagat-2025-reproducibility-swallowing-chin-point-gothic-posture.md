## One-line Summary

In-vivo clinical method comparison (n=30 edentulous/overdenture patients, custom digital intraoral gothic-arch tracer): digital gothic-arch tracing was the most reproducible centric-relation recording method (~0.98 / ~99% reliability) and posture-independent; light chin-point guidance was second (~0.79 / ~88%) and remained reliable in the supine posture; swallowing was least reproducible (~0.62 / ~77%) and dependable only upright — so chin-point guidance is preferable to swallowing chairside.

## 한줄요약

## Summary

The study compares three centric-relation (CR) recording techniques — **swallowing**, **light chin-point (jaw positioning) guidance**, and **gothic-arch tracing** — in 30 edentulous or overdenture patients, and tests how patient body posture (upright vs supine) affects each. Recording used a custom digital intraoral gothic-arch tracer (intraoral sensor + MAX232 digitizer + MATLAB) reading X/Y mandibular coordinates.

Reproducibility ranked: **gothic-arch tracing (~0.98 / ~99%) > chin-point guidance (~0.79 / ~88%) > swallowing (~0.62 / ~77%)**, all significant (p=0.000). Gothic-arch tracing showed excellent reproducibility with no significant change across postures (posture-independent). Chin-point guidance was reliable in the supine posture (Y-coordinate stable in the back position). Swallowing was most reliable only in the upright position. Clinical recommendation: **light chin-point guidance is more reproducible and posture-robust than swallowing**, making it the preferable chairside method over swallowing; gothic-arch tracing remains the overall reproducibility benchmark.

## Key Contributions

- Head-to-head, within-patient reproducibility comparison of three CR-recording methods with a digital quantitative readout.
- Adds the body-posture (upright vs supine) dimension — which method tolerates posture change.
- Ranks methods: gothic-arch tracing > chin-point guidance > swallowing.
- Practical recommendation: prefer chin-point guidance over swallowing chairside; chin-point guidance is dependable supine.

## Methodology

- **Design**: in-vivo within-subject method comparison, n=30 (well-formed ridges, adequate inter-arch space, no TMJ disorders).
- **Groups**: Group 1 = one edentulous arch + opposing overdenture (alloy copings over RCT teeth, permanent record bases + occlusal rims); Group 2 = both arches edentulous (denture bases from ZOE-impression secondary casts).
- **Device**: custom digital intraoral gothic-arch tracer — intraoral sensor pad + central bearing plate, extra-oral MAX232 digitizer, MATLAB analysis; outputs X/Y mandibular coordinates.
- **Methods**: (1) swallowing (lips sealed, oral cavity closed), (2) chin-point/light chin positioning guidance, (3) gothic-arch tracing.
- **Postures**: upright and supine (back); reproducibility coefficient + reliability % computed.
- **Ethics**: DY Patil Dental School (G-148/IECDYPDS/2024), informed consent.

## Results

Reproducibility (coefficient / reliability %, all p=0.000):

| Method | Coefficient | Reliability | Posture note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gothic-arch tracing** | **0.98** | **98.98%** | Excellent, **posture-independent** (no significant change upright↔supine) |
| **Chin-point guidance** | 0.79 | 88.26% | High consistency; **reliable supine** (Y-coordinate stable in back position) |
| **Swallowing** | 0.62 | 76.54% | Most reliable **only upright** |

- Cross-checks: Method 1 (swallowing) vs Method 3 ≈ 0.69 / 81.65%; Method 2 (chin-point) vs Method 3 ≈ 0.78–0.79 / 87.6–88.3%.
- **Bottom line**: gothic-arch tracing is the most reproducible and is posture-independent; among bedside guided methods, **chin-point guidance beats swallowing on reproducibility and is dependable supine**, so it is the preferable clinical choice over swallowing.
