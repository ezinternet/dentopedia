## One-line Summary

Bayesian NMA of 28 studies (n=1166, 2666 implants) found only 4-implant bar/telescopic overdentures showed significantly less marginal bone loss than 2-implant locator; all other attachment-implant combinations showed no clear peri-implant health differences.

## Summary

This Bayesian network meta-analysis (J Prosthodont Res 2022) is the first study to simultaneously compare all combinations of implant number (1–4) and attachment type (ball, bar, locator, telescopic, magnet) for peri-implant health in mandibular implant-supported overdentures (ODs). Twenty-eight studies (19 RCTs, 6 retrospective, 3 prospective) comprising 1166 patients receiving 2666 dental implants were included. Twelve intervention nodes were evaluated.

The overall pooled implant survival rate was 98.4%. The only statistically significant finding for marginal bone loss (MBL) was that 4-implant bar and 4-implant telescopic ODs produced significantly less MBL than 2-implant locator ODs. All other comparisons across MBL, PPD, plaque index, bleeding index, and gingival index were non-significant (P > 0.05). Rank probability testing favored 4 locator for least PPD, while 2 magnet and 2 bar ranked highest for plaque accumulation.

The conclusion supports the existing standard that 2-implant mandibular ODs are a valid minimum, but 4-implant designs with bar or telescopic attachments may offer a marginal bone-preservation advantage. The lack of overall superiority for most combinations suggests clinicians can individualize attachment selection based on cost, maintenance, and patient factors without compromising peri-implant health.

## Key Contributions

1. First NMA comparing all implant-number × attachment-type combinations simultaneously — overcomes limitation of prior pairwise MAs covering only 2–3 attachment types
2. Confirms 4-bar and 4-telescopic = lowest MBL; statistically significant only versus 2-locator
3. Overall ISR of 98.4% across diverse attachment systems (individual group range: 88.9%–100%)
4. 4-locator ranked best for PPD; 2-magnet and 2-bar ranked worst for plaque index
5. No significant inconsistency between direct and indirect evidence (node-splitting analysis)

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + Bayesian network meta-analysis
- **Search**: PubMed/Medline, Embase, Cochrane, Scopus — inception to October 2020
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and non-RCTs, ≥12 months follow-up, ≥5 patients/group, mandibular edentulous patients only, full-text English
- **Exclusion**: Crossover trials, mini-implants, maxillary or partial ODs
- **28 studies**: 19 RCTs + 6 retrospective + 3 prospective; n=1166, 2666 implants
- **12 intervention nodes**: 2 bar, 3 bar, 4 bar, 1 ball, 2 ball, 3 ball, 1 locator, 2 locator, 4 locator, 2 telescopic, 4 telescopic, 2 magnet
- **Outcomes**: MBL, PPD, PI, BI, GI (Mombelli index), ISR
- **Statistics**: Bayesian NMA (GeMTC/R/JAGS); WMD + 95% CrI; rank probability; node-splitting inconsistency test; comparison-adjusted funnel plots
- **Risk of bias**: 19 studies low, 9 moderate (MAOSE + STROBE + PRISMA criteria)

## Results

### Implant Survival
- Overall ISR: **98.4%** (43 failures in 2666 implants)
- 100% ISR: 4 bar (delayed), 3 bar, 2 locator (delayed), 4 telescopic, 2 magnet (immediate), 1 locator (delayed/early)
- Lowest ISR: 1 ball + immediate loading **88.9%**; 2 ball + immediate **92.9%**

### Marginal Bone Loss
- **Statistically significant**: 4 bar < 2 locator; 4 telescopic < 2 locator (P < 0.05)
- All other group comparisons: **not significant** (P > 0.05)
- Pairwise: 2 bar vs 2 ball MD = 0.081 mm (−0.152 to 0.314, NS); 2 ball vs 2 telescopic MD = −0.036 mm (−0.173 to 0.102, NS)
- Rank probability: **4 bar and 4 telescopic** ranked best (lowest MBL)

### Probing Depth
- No statistically significant differences among groups
- Rank: **4 locator** = least PPD

### Plaque / Bleeding / Gingival Index
- No statistically significant differences among groups
- Rank: **2 magnet** and **2 bar** = highest plaque accumulation

### Network Consistency
- Node-splitting: no significant inconsistency between direct and indirect evidence
