## One-line Summary

Clinical EEG study (n=21 complete denture wearers, duplicate dentures at −3 mm and +5 mm VDO): an immediate +5 mm increase in vertical dimension raised the EEG negative-mood indicator after gum chewing, and a −3 mm decrease lowered occlusal force — immediate VDO error perturbs both psychological state and bite force.

## 한줄요약

총의치 착용자 21명에서 수직고경(Vertical Dimension of Occlusion, VDO)을 −3 mm/+5 mm로 바꾼 복제 의치로 뇌파(EEG) 측정: +5 mm 증가 시 부정적 심리지표 유의 상승, −3 mm 감소 시 교합력 유의 감소 — 즉각적 VDO 오류가 심리·교합력 모두 교란.

## Summary

Long-term complete-denture use causes occlusal wear and jaw repositioning, altering the vertical dimension of occlusion (VDO). Beyond esthetics and chewing efficiency, VDO modulates occlusal force and — via the trigeminal pathway — central nervous system stimulation. This study asked whether acute VDO change measurably affects brain activity and psychological state in denture wearers, using electroencephalography (EEG). The answer is yes for an *increased* VDO (worsened mood index) and for a *decreased* VDO (reduced bite force), giving objective grounds for why accurate VDO determination matters clinically.

## Key Contributions

- First EEG-based demonstration that an acute VDO change in complete denture wearers shifts a psychological (negative-mood) indicator.
- Couples a prosthodontic parameter (VDO) to measurable neuro-psychological and biomechanical outcomes.

## Methodology

- **Design**: within-subject experimental, n=21 regular complete-denture wearers (12 M / 9 F, mean 76.6 y).
- **Dentures**: each subject's original denture duplicated at **−3 mm** and **+5 mm** VDO.
- **Outcomes**: EEG (negative-indicator score; D α stable-neuronal-activity index) and occlusal force, before and after gum chewing with each denture.
- **Analysis**: Wilcoxon signed-rank, α = 0.05.

## Results

- **+5 mm (increased VDO)** → significant rise in the EEG negative-mood indicator after chewing (p < 0.05).
- **D α** → no significant change with any denture (p > 0.05).
- **−3 mm (decreased VDO)** → significant decrease in occlusal force vs original denture (p < 0.05).
- **Bottom line**: immediate VDO changes influence psychological condition (over-opening) and occlusal force (over-closing); supports careful VDO setting and gradual adaptation when altering it.
