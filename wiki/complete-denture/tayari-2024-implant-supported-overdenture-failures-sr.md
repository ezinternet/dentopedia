## One-line Summary
PRISMA systematic review of 20 studies (2018–2023) identifying risk factors for implant-supported overdenture failures: maxillary arch, fewer implants, immediate loading, poor systemic health, and absence of metal-reinforced prosthesis design each independently elevate failure rates.

## 한줄요약
PRISMA 체계적 문헌고찰 (20편, 2018–2023): 임플란트 지지 피개의치 실패 위험인자 — 상악 악궁, 임플란트 수 부족, 즉시 부하, 불량 전신 상태, 금속 보강 없는 의치 기저부 설계가 각각 독립적으로 실패율을 높인다.

## Summary
This PRISMA-compliant systematic review (Journal of Indian Prosthodontic Society, 2024) by Tayari et al. from the University of Monastir synthesizes evidence on variables associated with failure of implant-supported overdentures (IODs). Searching MEDLINE/PubMed, Cochrane, and LILACS for studies published between 2018 and 2023, the authors retained 20 articles — 13 RCTs, 1 prospective clinical trial, 3 prospective cohorts, 2 retrospective studies, and 1 cross-sectional study. Risk of bias was assessed using the Cochrane Collaboration tool.

The review distinguishes implant failure (osseointegration defect, implant loss/fracture, advanced peri-implantitis) from prosthetic failure (attachment/screw fracture/loss, attachment overload due to axial discrepancy, unexplained prosthetic tooth or base fracture, patient dissatisfaction).

## Key Contributions
- Provides a multi-domain risk factor map for IOD failure covering patient factors, arch anatomy, implant number, loading protocol, attachment type, and prosthetic design.
- Reaffirms the McGill/York consensus that mandibular 2-implant OD is a robust standard of care while quantifying conditions under which it fails.
- Highlights the inverse relationship between implant failure and prosthetic base failure in maxillary IODs: fewer implants → higher implant failure but lower prosthetic failure; more implants → lower implant failure but more prosthetic maintenance.
- Identifies metal framework reinforcement and total palatal coverage as key protective design features for maxillary IODs.

## Methodology
- **Databases**: MEDLINE (PubMed), Cochrane Central, LILACS + manual search
- **Date range**: January 2018 – March 2023
- **PICO**: Edentulous patients (maxillary/mandibular) receiving implant-supported complete removable prostheses; outcome: identifying failure risk factors vs conventional complete dentures
- **Study designs included**: RCT, prospective CT, observational study (cross-sectional, prospective, retrospective)
- **Excluded**: case reports/series, reviews/meta-analyses, mini-implant studies, partial edentulism, fixed implant prostheses
- **Screened**: 188 records → 20 included (13 RCT, 1 prospective CT, 3 prospective, 2 retrospective, 1 cross-sectional)
- **RoB assessment**: Cochrane Collaboration tool (low/unclear/high risk classification)

## Results

### Implant Failure Rates by Arch and Implant Number
| Scenario | Implant Failure Rate |
|---|---|
| Single mandibular implant | 0%–8.9% |
| Two mandibular implants | 0%–2.5% |
| Maxillary 4-implant OD (best case) | ~5% average |
| Maxillary 4-implant + locator (worst cases) | 13.8%–27.5% |
| Maxillary 4-implant + GBR (Kappel, no bone graft) | 18.9% |

- Average maxillary implant failure rate ~5% vs mandibular ~3.3%
- Single midline mandibular implant: acceptable performance with locator attachment; all studies reported 0% implant failure at 10-year follow-up when well-selected

### Prosthetic Failure Rates
- Single-implant mandibular OD (ball attachment): prosthetic failure 36.3%–72%, mainly base fractures
- Two-implant mandibular OD with bar: prosthetic failure ~12.7%
- Maxillary 4-implant + bar + total palatal coverage + metal framework: only 8.33% prosthetic failure
- Maxillary 4-implant + partial palatal coverage without metal reinforcement: 60%–70% prosthetic failure
- 6-implant maxillary OD: higher prosthetic maintenance burden but lower implant failure

### Key Risk Factors Identified

**1. Edentulous arch (악궁 유형)**
- Maxillary bone has lower density (Type III/IV) → higher implant failure risk
- Mandibular midline offers denser cortical bone with more predictable osseointegration

**2. Number of implants (임플란트 수)**
- Minimum 2 implants for mandibular IOD; ≥4 for maxillary IOD
- More implants reduce per-implant load but may increase total prosthetic maintenance events

**3. Loading protocol (부하 프로토콜)**
- Immediate loading increases failure risk: all failures in Kern's trial occurred within first 12 weeks
- Immediate incorporation of attachments into resin bases when tissues are swollen → thin resin → higher fracture risk
- Recommended: delayed loading in compromised hosts; if immediate loading, use metal reinforcement and thermopolymerizable resin

**4. Patient systemic health (환자 전신 상태)**
- Diabetes, smoking, bisphosphonates, head/neck irradiation — all elevate implant failure risk
- Compromised hosts warrant conventional loading and closer recall intervals

**5. Attachment type (부착 시스템 종류)**
- Ball attachment (single implant): highest prosthetic failure rate (40% with single maxillary implant)
- Bar attachment: better load distribution; lower peri-implantitis rate vs locator in maxilla (Slot et al.)
- Locator: higher peri-implantitis incidence reported; convenient but more maintenance
- Telescopic crown locator: lowest plaque index and gingival index in one RCT (ElSyad 2018)

**6. Prosthetic design (의치 설계)**
- Metal framework reinforcement: consensus across authors as essential for fracture prevention
- Total palatal coverage vs partial: partial coverage increases stress on implants without bar splinting
- Maxillary prosthesis without metal framework: markedly higher fracture rates (confirmed by Osman SR)

**7. Antagonist arch**
- Natural dentition antagonist increases failure risk for maxillary IODs (Bouhy 2020)
- No significant risk for mandibular IODs from natural dentition antagonists (Ohkubo/Baek SR)

**8. Bone grafting and GBR**
- GBR prior to implant placement reduced implant failure: rates of 4.7%, 1.7%, and 0% in three studies
- Minimally invasive augmentation (osteotome/internal sinus lift) without bone graft: failure rate 18.9%

### Peri-implant Tissue Complications
- Peri-implant mucositis and peri-implantitis were reported across all study types
- Bar attachment groups showed lower peri-implantitis incidence than locator groups
- Long-term (10 years) maxillary IOD on 6 implants: peri-implant mucositis 52.9%–65%, peri-implantitis 20%–38.5%
- Probing depths >5 mm and bone loss >3 mm recorded in some implants even when prostheses survived
