## Summary

Taylor et al. (2021) conducted the first systematic review and meta-analysis specifically examining the longevity of complete dentures. Searching four electronic databases, they identified 42 eligible studies (out of 21,607 abstracts screened) — all rated low or very low risk of bias using the AXIS tool. Using weighted means (Bland & Kerry) and random-effects pooled failure proportions (DerSimonian & Laird), they established that complete dentures fabricated primarily in university settings last a weighted mean of 10.1 ± 4.0 years overall. Maxillary dentures lasted longer (10.3 yr) than mandibular dentures (8.6 yr), a difference attributed to the greater severity of residual ridge resorption in the mandible. Failure proportions increased progressively from 5% at ≤2 years to 41% at ≥10 years, confirming that denture age is a key predictor of failure.

## Key Contributions

1. **First pooled longevity estimate** for complete dentures — provides evidence-based benchmarks for clinician counseling on denture replacement timing.
2. **Arch-specific data**: maxillary 10.3 ± 3.8 yr vs. mandibular 8.6 ± 2.6 yr — mandibular dentures require earlier replacement due to greater alveolar resorption and reduced tissue support.
3. **Time-dependent failure rates**: failure doubles from ~5% at 2 years to 12% at 5–6 years and exceeds 40% at 10+ years — supports proactive replacement counseling after 10 years of service.
4. **High-quality evidence base**: 24 studies low RoB, 18 very low RoB by AXIS tool.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA-compliant systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Databases**: MEDLINE, CINAHL (EBSCO), Dentistry and Oral Sciences Database, Cochrane Library
- **Screening**: 21,607 abstracts → 273 inclusions → 42 final studies
- **Quality tool**: AXIS (cross-sectional critical appraisal tool)
- **Statistics**:
  - Weighted means and SDs (Bland & Kerry, 1998)
  - Random-effects meta-analysis for failure proportions (DerSimonian & Laird, 1986)
  - I² for heterogeneity assessment
- **Setting**: Predominantly university/academic dental clinics; private-practice data minimal

## Results

**Weighted mean longevity:**

| Arch | Weighted mean (yr) | SD (yr) |
|---|---|---|
| Maxillary | 10.3 | 3.8 |
| Mandibular | 8.6 | 2.6 |
| Both arches | 10.8 | 4.7 |
| Overall | 10.1 | 4.0 |

**Pooled failure proportions (random-effects):**

| Follow-up | Failure proportion | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| ≤2 years | 0.05 | 0.00–0.10 |
| 5–6 years | 0.12 | 0.08–0.16 |
| ≥10 years | 0.41 | 0.28–0.53 |

**Key findings:**
- Maxillary dentures outlast mandibular dentures consistently across included studies
- Failure rate increases progressively with denture age — age is the dominant failure predictor
- University-fabricated dentures may have higher longevity than community/private-practice dentures (fabrication quality, supervision, follow-up)
- Heterogeneity was noted; no digital/milled complete dentures were included (studies predated widespread CAD-CAM adoption)
