## One-line Summary

SR+MA (3 RCTs) demonstrating microwave disinfection of complete dentures is as effective as topical nystatin for treating denture stomatitis, with low GRADE certainty of evidence.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated the effectiveness of microwave disinfection of complete dentures compared to topical antifungal therapy (nystatin) for the treatment of denture stomatitis (의치성 구내염, DS). Six databases were searched, yielding 1524 records; after deduplication, 816 remained, with 3 studies included for qualitative synthesis and 2 for meta-analysis. Random-effects meta-analysis was applied. All included studies reported significant reductions in Candida counts and clinical signs within both treatment groups. Meta-analysis found no statistically significant differences between microwave disinfection and nystatin for Candida counts on dentures or for frequency of clinical signs of stomatitis (p > .05). The sole significant difference favored microwave disinfection for Candida quantification on the palate within 90 days (SMD = 0.47, 95% CI 0.02–0.91). GRADE certainty was rated as low. The authors conclude microwave disinfection can be considered as efficient as antifungal therapy, though further well-designed RCTs are needed.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes microwave disinfection as a viable non-pharmacological alternative to nystatin for denture stomatitis management
- Quantifies the equivalence using random-effects meta-analysis with SMD effect sizes
- Applies GRADE framework, providing transparent evidence quality assessment (low certainty)
- Highlights a specific advantage of microwave disinfection on palatal Candida reduction at 90 days
- Relevant for patients where antifungal therapy is contraindicated or where drug resistance is a concern

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA-based)
- **PICO**: P = complete denture wearers with denture stomatitis; I = microwave disinfection; C = topical antifungal (nystatin); O = Candida counts (denture + palate), clinical DS signs
- **Databases**: 6 databases searched
- **Records**: 1524 identified → 816 after deduplication → 3 included (qualitative); 2 for meta-analysis
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects meta-analysis
- **Effect measure**: Standardized Mean Difference (SMD) with 95% confidence intervals
- **Evidence quality**: GRADE approach

## Results

| Outcome | SMD (95% CI) | p-value | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Candida – palate (≤90 days) | 0.47 (0.02–0.91) | < .05 | Microwave significantly better |
| Candida – denture surface | Not significant | > .05 | Equivalent |
| Clinical signs of DS | Not significant | > .05 | Equivalent |

- Both microwave disinfection and nystatin produced significant within-group reductions in all outcomes
- GRADE certainty: **low** across all outcomes
- Clinical implication: Microwave disinfection is as effective as antifungal therapy and may be preferred as a drug-free approach
