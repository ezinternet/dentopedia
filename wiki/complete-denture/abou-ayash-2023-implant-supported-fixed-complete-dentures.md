## Summary

This 2023 systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO: CRD42022269277) examined the treatment effects of implant-supported fixed complete dentures (IFCDs) and implant overdentures (IODs) on dental patient-reported outcomes (dPROs) in fully edentulous patients. Twenty-eight prospective studies involving 1457 patients were included. Both prosthesis types produced large effect sizes — IFCD: Hedges' g = 1.68 (95% CI: 1.15–2.20) and IOD: g = 1.26 (95% CI: 0.99–1.52) — with no statistically significant overall difference (p = .165). The only dPRO domain where IFCDs significantly outperformed IODs was denture stability (ES difference: 2.37; p = .032). Among mandibular IODs, two-implant designs produced moderately higher dPROs than single-implant designs (ES difference: 0.73; p < .001). Attachment type (ball vs bar) and follow-up duration did not significantly moderate outcomes.

## Key Contributions

1. **Quantified large treatment effects** for both IFCDs and IODs using Hedges' g — both prosthesis types substantially improve OHRQoL beyond the pre-treatment (edentulous/conventional denture) baseline
2. **No overall PRO superiority** of fixed over removable implant prosthetics, challenging assumptions that "fixed = better patient experience"
3. **Stability is the decisive differentiator** — if a patient specifically seeks stability, IFCD is the evidence-based recommendation
4. **Two-implant advantage for mandibular IODs** — adding a second implant to a single-implant IOD produces a clinically meaningful PRO improvement
5. **Attachment type does not affect PROs** — ball vs bar retention delivers equivalent patient satisfaction

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis; PRISMA-compliant
- **Databases**: Medline, Embase, CINAHL, Cochrane Library, PubMed Central, Web of Science, ClinicalTrials.gov (searched January 2022)
- **Inclusion**: Prospective studies; edentulous patients; IFCD or IOD; validated dPROMs; pre- and post-treatment data; n ≥ 10; follow-up ≥ 1 year
- **Outcome instruments**: OHIP (multiple versions), VAS satisfaction scales
- **Effect size**: Hedges' g with 95% CI; random-effects models
- **Subgroups**: Prosthesis type, attachment system (IODs), implant number (IODs), follow-up duration
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB tool (RCTs), ROBINS-I (non-randomized prospective)
- **Total records**: 1608 initially identified → 28 studies included

## Results

| Outcome | Group | Hedges' g | 95% CI | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Overall dPRO | IFCD | 1.68 | 1.15–2.20 | — |
| Overall dPRO | IOD | 1.26 | 0.99–1.52 | — |
| IFCD vs IOD | Difference | NS | — | .165 |
| Denture stability | IFCD vs IOD | 2.37 | 0.21–4.54 | .032 |
| IOD: 2 vs 1 implant | Mandibular | 0.73 | 0.34–1.12 | <.001 |
| Attachment type | Balls vs bars | NS | — | NS |
| Follow-up time | Moderator | NS | — | NS |

**Clinical interpretation**:
- Both treatment modalities achieve large (g > 0.8) positive effects on OHRQoL
- Patient counseling should center on stability preference rather than assuming fixed prostheses are universally superior
- For mandibular overdentures, two-implant protocols are strongly supported by PRO evidence
- Attachment selection can be driven by clinical and cost factors, not PRO expectations
