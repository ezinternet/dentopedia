## One-line Summary

Updated narrative review (J Oral Rehabil 2017) classifying OVD-determination methods for complete-denture patients into pre-extraction (intra-oral measurement, profile tracing, cephalometry) and post-extraction (rest position, facial esthetics, swallowing, craniofacial-landmark measurement, cephalometry, phonetics, existing dentures), concluding all are empirical and no single method is accurate, so techniques should be combined.

## Summary

A frequently-cited Journal of Oral Rehabilitation review that organizes the determination of **occlusal vertical dimension (OVD)** for **complete-denture (edentulous)** patients into a clean two-stream framework. Because the teeth are gone, the clinician must reconstruct a vertical jaw relation that was previously defined by the natural dentition — and this paper lays out the available routes and their weaknesses. It is the conceptual anchor of the `complete-denture` category and the predecessor cited by the 2025 Cureus Part II review.

## Key Contributions

- **(i) Pre-extraction methods** — capture the natural-dentition OVD and transfer it to the new denture, mainly by **intra-oral measurements, profile tracing, and cephalometric analysis**.
- **(ii) Post-extraction methods** — reconstruct OVD after tooth loss using **mandibular rest position, facial (esthetic) appearance, swallowing pattern, craniofacial-landmark measurements, cephalometric analysis, phonetics, and existing dentures**.
- Critical appraisal: every technique has merit and clinical utility but is **empirical, controversial, and lacks scientific support**; **no single accurate method** exists.

## Methodology

Narrative review of the OVD-determination literature, structured by the pre-/post-extraction dichotomy, with a large reference base spanning pre-extraction records, lingual-frenum guides, tooth-measurement methods, Willis-gauge facial measurement, cephalometric estimation, and phonetic/closest-speaking-space methods. No meta-analysis or pooled effect sizes. (Wiki page grounded in the abstract + reference list of the web-print PDF.)

## Results

- All methods carry trade-offs: pre-extraction records demand foresight and are often unavailable in long-term edentulous patients; post-extraction physiologic and esthetic methods are subjective and population-dependent.
- **Clinical recommendation:** combine multiple techniques rather than relying on any single one to best approximate OVD for complete dentures.
