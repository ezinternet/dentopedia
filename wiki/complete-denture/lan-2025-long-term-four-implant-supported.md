## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 studies (5,568 implants, ≥5y) comparing maxillary four-implant overdentures vs fixed complete dentures: fixed restorations showed higher implant (98.5%) and prosthesis (99.9%) survival; overdentures showed less marginal bone loss (−0.27 vs −1.20 mm).

## Summary

This SR+MA by Lan et al. (2025) synthesizes medium-to-long-term outcomes for maxillary four-implant-supported overdentures (IODs) and full-arch fixed restorations (IFRs). Among 16 eligible studies (5,568 implants), both modalities showed clinically acceptable outcomes after ≥5 years. Fixed complete dentures demonstrated superior implant survival (98.5% vs 94.5%) and prosthesis survival (99.9% vs 85.0%), but were associated with greater peri-implant marginal bone loss (−1.20 vs −0.27 mm). Overdentures accumulated more mechanical complications, primarily retention loss (0.34/patient), while fixed restorations mainly experienced acrylic tooth dislodgment/fracture (0.09/patient). Both treatment modalities are deemed clinically predictable at medium-term.

## Key Contributions

1. **Comparative evidence for maxillary four-implant designs**: First SR+MA directly comparing IOD vs IFR in the maxilla with ≥5-year follow-up, quantifying survival, MBL, and complications.
2. **Fixed vs removable trade-off**: IFRs offer superior implant and prosthesis survival; IODs preserve crestal bone and have lower prosthesis loss risk but higher maintenance burden.
3. **Complication profiling**: Retention loss dominates IOD complications; acrylic tooth fracture/dislodgment dominates IFR complications — both are manageable but have different clinical impact.
4. **Clinical predictability confirmed**: Despite inter-study heterogeneity, both approaches yield predictable medium-term results.

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Search databases**: PubMed, Embase, Cochrane + manual search
- **Inclusion criteria**: ≥10 maxillary edentulous patients, four-implant IOD or IFR, ≥5-year follow-up
- **Studies included**: 16 total (9 IOD studies, 7 IFR studies); 5,568 implants total
- **Primary outcome**: Implant survival rate (ISR)
- **Secondary outcomes**: Prosthesis survival, marginal bone loss (MBL), complications
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 (RCTs), Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (observational studies)
- **Subgroup analyses**: Attachment system type and study type (for IOD)

## Results

### Implant Survival Rate
| Prosthesis | ISR | 95% CI | I² |
|---|---|---|---|
| IOD (overdenture) | 94.5% | 92.1–96.9% | 84.22% |
| IFR (fixed) | 98.5% | 97.4–99.5% | 77.88% |

### Prosthesis Survival Rate
| Prosthesis | Survival |
|---|---|
| IOD | 85.0% |
| IFR | 99.9% |

### Marginal Bone Loss at 5 Years
| Prosthesis | MBL (mean ± SD) |
|---|---|
| IOD | −0.27 ± 1.31 mm |
| IFR | −1.20 ± 0.76 mm |

### Complications
- **IOD**: Retention loss was the most common (0.34 events/patient)
- **IFR**: Acrylic tooth dislodgment or fracture (0.09 events/patient)

### Clinical Interpretation
- IFRs are preferable when prioritizing implant and prosthesis longevity
- IODs are preferable when peri-implant bone preservation is paramount or when cost/surgical complexity favors a removable design
- Both options achieve predictable medium-term clinical performance in the maxillary arch
