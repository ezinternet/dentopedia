## One-line Summary

SR+MA (20 of 22 analytical cross-sectional studies, n=325 completely edentulous patients) comparing extraoral Gothic arch tracing against four methods of measuring horizontal condylar guidance (HCG) angle: cephalogram and panoramic radiograph (OPG) give significantly HIGHER angles (cephalogram pooled MD ≈ +10.2–10.7°, OPG ≈ +5.1°, both p<0.05 bilaterally) while CBCT and protrusive interocclusal wax record show NO significant difference — authors name OPG the best accurate alternative for programming the semi-adjustable articulator.

## 한줄요약

무치악 환자(n=325) 수평과로각(Horizontal Condylar Guidance, HCG) 측정에서 구외 고딕아치 트레이싱(extraoral Gothic arch tracing)을 4가지 방법과 비교한 SR+MA(22편 중 20편 메타분석): 측방두부규격(cephalogram)·파노라마(OPG)는 유의하게 큰 값(cephalogram 약 +10.2~10.7°, OPG 약 +5.1°, 양측 p<0.05), CBCT·교합간 왁스기록은 차이 없음 — 반조절성 교합기(semi-adjustable articulator) 프로그래밍에 OPG가 가장 정확한 대안.

## Summary

Condylar guidance — the sagittal inclination of the condyle traversing the glenoid fossa during protrusion — is a patient-derived occlusal determinant that programs the horizontal condylar element of a semi-adjustable articulator, and an accurate HCG value is needed to develop balanced complete-denture occlusion free of interferences. Extraoral Gothic arch (arrowhead) tracing via a set protrusive interocclusal record is the long-standing clinical reference method, but it is technique-sensitive (denture-base instability, soft-tissue compressibility, patient neuromuscular coordination, recording-medium properties). This SR+MA (PRISMA 2020; PROSPERO CRD42022283974) asks which alternative methods — panoramic radiograph (OPG), cephalogram, CBCT, and protrusive interocclusal wax record — best agree with or improve on Gothic arch tracing for the edentulous patient.

Across 22 studies (n=325; mostly India), cephalogram and OPG produced systematically and significantly higher HCG angles than Gothic arch tracing, whereas CBCT and the wax record did not differ significantly. The authors conclude the panoramic radiograph is the best practical accurate alternative for capturing and transferring the HCG angle to a semi-adjustable articulator.

## Key Contributions

- First meta-analytic quantification of the HCG-angle gap between extraoral Gothic arch tracing and four alternatives in completely edentulous patients.
- Side-specific pooled mean differences (right and left) for each comparator, with model selection driven by heterogeneity (I²).
- A clinical recommendation: OPG (and cephalogram) can substitute for the technique-sensitive clinical tracer when programming the articulator.

## Methodology

- **Design**: SR+MA, PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO CRD42022283974.
- **Search**: EBSCOhost, Cochrane Library, PubMed/MEDLINE, Google Scholar; English, Jan 2005–Dec 2022. Inter-rater Cohen kappa = 0.90.
- **PICOS**: completely edentulous patients >35 y, healthy ridges; comparison = extraoral Gothic arch tracing via protrusive interocclusal record; outcome = HCG angle.
- **Screening**: 513 records → 69 full-text → 22 included (qualitative) → 20 included (meta-analysis). 325 participants; 19 India / 2 Egypt / 1 Nepal; 21/22 used the Hanau Wide Vue semi-adjustable articulator.
- **Risk of bias**: Newcastle–Ottawa (modified case–control scale) — 20 "good," 2 "fair."
- **Synthesis**: RevMan 5.4; forest plots (mean/SD/total, 95% CI); I²>50% → random effect, <50% → fixed effect; funnel plot for publication bias.

## Results

Pooled mean difference (comparator − Gothic arch tracing), 95% CI, both sides:

| Comparator | n studies | Right side MD [95% CI], p (I²) | Left side MD [95% CI], p (I²) | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Panoramic radiograph (OPG)** | 15 | **+5.08 [2.17, 7], p<0.05** (I²=89%) | **+5.07 [1.95, 8.18], p<0.05** (I²=90%) | YES — OPG higher |
| **Cephalogram** | 2 | **+10.65 [8.81, 12.49], p<0.05** (I²=0%) | **+10.24 [8.65, 11.83], p<0.001** (I²=0%) | YES — cephalogram higher |
| CBCT | 5 | +4.39 [−6.10, 14.87], p=0.41 (I²=99%) | +4.05 [−3.74, 11.84], p=0.31 (I²=98%) | NO |
| Protrusive interocclusal wax record | 4 | −0.45 [−9.62, 8.72], p=0.92 (I²=98%) | −1.21 [−7.86, 5.43], p=0.72 (I²=95%) | NO |

- **Significant**: cephalogram and OPG yield higher HCG angles than the clinical tracer, bilaterally (cephalogram pool had I²=0%, the tightest estimate).
- **Not significant**: CBCT and the protrusive wax record vs Gothic arch tracing — but both pools carry very high heterogeneity (I²=95–99%), so "no difference" is weakly supported.
- **Conclusion**: by mean difference and study count, the panoramic radiograph is judged the best method to obtain a higher/more accurate HCG angle vs extraoral Gothic arch tracing for programming the semi-adjustable articulator.

**Caveat**: "accuracy" here means agreement against the Gothic arch tracing reference rather than a true anatomical gold standard, so a "higher" reading is not automatically "more correct." The cephalogram pool rests on only 2 studies, and India-heavy sampling (19/22) limits generalizability.
