## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 39 RCTs/CCTs showing denture adhesives significantly improve complete denture retention (SMD 1.34), bite force (SMD 0.98), and masticatory performance (SMD 0.72).

## 한줄요약

39개 RCT/CCT 메타분석: 의치접착제는 완전의치 유지력(SMD 1.34), 최대교합력(SMD 0.98), 저작기능(SMD 0.72)을 유의하게 향상시킴.

## Summary

This is the first systematic review and meta-analysis restricted to randomized and non-randomized controlled clinical trials (RCTs and CCTs) evaluating denture adhesives (DAs) specifically for complete dentures (CDs). Searching Medline, Embase, and CENTRAL up to March 2020, the authors identified 39 eligible studies (43 articles) from 1,729 records. Meta-analysis demonstrated that DAs provide statistically and clinically significant improvements in denture retention (SMD 1.34), maximum bite force (SMD 0.98), and masticatory performance (SMD 0.72). The study concludes that DAs are a viable conservative management option for complete denture wearers experiencing declining retention and masticatory function.

## Key Contributions

- First SR+MA limited to RCTs/CCTs (prior 2015 SR included observational studies and mixed prosthesis types without meta-analysis)
- Quantified effect sizes for three primary mastication-related outcomes using SMD
- Covered all DA formulation types: soluble (cream, paste, gel, powder) and insoluble (strip, pad, wafer, cushion)
- Provided clinical recommendations for prosthodontists and general practitioners on DA use in complete dentures

## Methodology

- **Study types**: RCTs (n=23) and CCTs (n=16); 2 multicenter trials
- **Databases**: Medline, Embase, CENTRAL + gray literature; search cutoff March 2020
- **Records**: 1,729 → 77 full-text reviewed → 39 studies included (43 articles)
- **Primary outcomes**: denture retention, maximum bite force, masticatory efficiency (all objectively measured)
- **Secondary outcomes**: other objective variables + patient-reported outcomes (OHRQoL, oral microorganisms)
- **Effect size**: Standardized Mean Difference (SMD) with random-effects model; sensitivity analysis conducted
- **DA types**: Active ingredients CMC (carboxymethyl cellulose) and PVM-MA (polyvinyl methyl ether/maleic acid) in soluble forms; water-activated fabric mesh in insoluble forms
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42020164316

## Results

| Outcome | SMD | 95% CI | P-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Denture retention | 1.34 | 0.89–1.79 | <0.001 |
| Maximum bite force | 0.98 | 0.50–1.47 | <0.001 |
| Masticatory performance | 0.72 | 0.23–1.22 | 0.004 |

- Retention showed the largest effect (SMD 1.34 = large effect), consistent with the primary mechanism of DA action
- Bite force (SMD 0.98) and masticatory performance (SMD 0.72) also significantly improved but with smaller effect sizes
- Heterogeneity was substantial across studies, attributed to differences in DA type, denture quality, ridge condition, and measurement method
- Patient-reported outcomes (comfort, confidence, satisfaction) generally improved; oral microorganism data were inconclusive
- Clinical indications: patients with xerostomia, compromised anatomic structures, frail/handicapped patients, and during the CD adaptation period
