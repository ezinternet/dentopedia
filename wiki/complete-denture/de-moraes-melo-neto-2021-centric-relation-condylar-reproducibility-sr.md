## One-line Summary

Systematic review (6 low-quality clinical studies, complete-dentition non-TMD subjects) comparing centric-relation recording techniques *within the same method*: all 6 compared only bimanual manipulation vs chin-point guidance (guided method) — 3 favored bimanual, 2 equivalent, 1 favored chin-point — so bimanual manipulation is the more recommended CR technique.

## 한줄요약

체계적 문헌고찰(6편, 모두 저질·완전치열·비TMD)로 중심위(Centric Relation, CR) 획득 술식을 동일 방법 내에서 비교 — 6편 모두 유도법의 양손조작(Bimanual) 대 턱끝유도(Chin-point guidance)만 비교, 3편 양손조작 우세·2편 동등·1편 턱끝유도 우세 → 과두위치 재현성에서 양손조작법이 더 권장됨.

## Summary

Centric relation (CR) is the maxillomandibular reference position in which the condyles sit anterior-superior against the posterior slopes of the articular eminences — a clinically useful, *repeatable* reference used for prosthetic rehabilitation, TMD therapy, and occlusal/orthodontic planning. CR-recording techniques fall into three method families: **guided** (bimanual manipulation, chin-point guidance), **graphic** (intraoral/extraoral gothic-arch tracing), and **physiologic** (swallowing, tongue retrusion). This PRISMA systematic review (PROSPERO CRD42018097285) asked which technique *within each method* most reproducibly captures the condylar position.

Of 1692 records, 6 low-quality clinical studies met inclusion. Critically, **every included study compared only bimanual manipulation vs chin-point guidance** (the guided method) — there were no studies comparing the two graphic techniques or the two physiologic techniques, and none enrolled edentulous patients. Across the 6 studies, the bimanual technique was the more reproducible in 3, equivalent in 2, and inferior in only 1. The authors conclude that for dentate, non-TMD patients, **bimanual manipulation is the more recommended CR technique** — plausibly because two-handed mandibular stabilization reduces manipulation error versus one-handed chin-point guidance.

## Key Contributions

- Synthesizes the *within-method* CR-technique evidence and shows it is almost entirely the guided method (bimanual vs chin-point).
- Provides a technique-level evidence anchor for the horizontal/centric jaw-relation record: **bimanual ≥ chin-point** for condylar-position reproducibility.
- Maps the evidence gaps: no head-to-head graphic (gothic-arch) or physiologic comparisons, and no edentulous data.

## Methodology

- **Design**: PRISMA systematic review; PROSPERO CRD42018097285; qualitative synthesis (no meta-analysis — device heterogeneity prevented pooling).
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, SciELO, Scopus, Web of Science (up to May 5, 2021).
- **Inclusion**: English clinical studies; non-TMD subjects; complete/near-complete dentition or complete edentulism; ≥5 subjects; ≥2 same-method CR techniques compared on reproducibility.
- **Techniques considered** — guided: bimanual, chin-point (traditional + three-finger); graphic: intraoral/extraoral gothic arch; physiologic: swallowing, tongue retrusion.
- **Quality**: Jadad scale; reviewer agreement by Kappa (1.00 at both screening stages).

## Results

- **Flow**: 1692 → 12 full-text → **6 included**. All 6 **low quality** (Jadad ≤2); only 2/6 randomized; double-blinding not applicable.
- **Population**: all complete dentition; **none edentulous**.
- **Only comparison present**: bimanual vs chin-point guidance (guided method).

| Study | n | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Kantor et al | 15 | **Bimanual > chin-point** |
| Hobo & Iwata | 10 | **Bimanual > chin-point** |
| Keshvad & Winstanley | 14 | **Bimanual > chin-point** |
| Simon & Nicholls | 5 | No difference |
| Galeković et al | 32 | No difference |
| Teo & Wise | 7 | Chin-point > bimanual |

- **Tally**: 3 bimanual-superior · 2 equivalent · 1 chin-point-superior → **bimanual most often superior or equivalent**.
- **Clinical bottom line**: for dentate, non-TMD patients, **bimanual manipulation is preferred** for reproducible CR recording.
- **Method caveats**: standardize a single operator, patient position (supine pushes the mandible more posterior via gravity), and time-of-day; pre-record with a Lucia jig (5–20 min across studies) to deprogram proprioceptive memory and relax muscles. Guided techniques are more operator-dependent than graphic/physiologic ones, so operator experience matters.
