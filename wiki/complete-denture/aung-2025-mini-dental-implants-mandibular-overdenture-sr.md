## One-line Summary
SR+MA (6 RCTs, 101 patients, 234 mini dental implants): in mini dental implant (MDI, <3 mm) retained mandibular overdentures, ball, bar, and ERA® attachments yield equivalent marginal bone loss (MBL) while telescopic attachments cause significantly greater MBL (p<0.05); maximum bite force and implant survival do not differ by attachment, and ball attachments have the lowest 1-year prosthetic maintenance.

## Summary
Mini dental implants (MDI, <3 mm diameter) are a less invasive, lower-cost alternative to conventional implants for atrophic mandibular ridges in edentulous patients, particularly the elderly or medically compromised. However, the optimal attachment system for MDI-retained overdentures has been less studied than for full-diameter implants. This 2025 PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42024610018) pooled six RCTs (101 patients, 234 mini-implants) up to February 2025.

The primary outcome was marginal bone loss (MBL). Ball, bar, and ERA® attachments produced statistically equivalent MBL; **telescopic attachments produced significantly more MBL (p<0.05)** — a notable finding because telescopic systems perform well on full implants. Maximum bite force did not differ by attachment. Implant survival remained high across attachments. Prosthetic maintenance over 1-year follow-up was lowest for ball attachments (two events: denture repair and nylon cap replacement), while telescopic required multiple repairs.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA focused on MDI overdenture attachment systems.
- Reveals that **attachment behavior on MDIs is not the same as on conventional implants** — telescopic is worse on MDIs.
- Anchors ball attachment as the low-maintenance default for MDI-OD.

## Methodology
- Databases: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Scopus (up to 25 Feb 2025).
- Inclusion: RCTs comparing ball vs other attachment in MDI overdentures.
- 6 RCTs retained: 3 low RoB, 3 some-concern (Cochrane tool).
- Quantitative meta-analysis for MBL and bite force.

## Results
| Comparison | Outcome | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Ball vs Bar vs ERA® | MBL | NS (statistically equivalent) |
| **Telescopic vs others** | **MBL** | **Telescopic significantly higher (p<0.05)** |
| All attachments | Max bite force | NS |
| All attachments | Implant survival | High, NS by attachment |
| Ball | 1-yr maintenance events | **2 (lowest)** |
| Telescopic | 1-yr maintenance events | Multiple (repairs, nylon caps) |

Clinical implication: on mini dental implants (<3 mm), **ball or bar or ERA® are the preferred choices**, with ball offering the simplest maintenance profile. **Avoid telescopic on MDIs** in routine practice.
