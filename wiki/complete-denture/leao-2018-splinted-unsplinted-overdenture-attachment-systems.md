## One-line Summary

## Summary

This 2018 PRISMA-compliant SR+MA from the Journal of Oral Rehabilitation addressed a focused PICO question: do splinted overdenture attachment systems produce better clinical results than unsplinted systems? Nine eligible studies (RCTs and prospective studies, ≥10 participants, ≥6-month follow-up, comparing both systems within the same study) were identified from PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, and Cochrane (search through October 2017). The pooled sample comprised 984 implants placed in 380 patients (mean age 62.8 years).

Meta-analysis demonstrated no statistically significant differences between splinted and unsplinted systems on any of the three primary outcomes. Marginal bone loss showed a mean difference of −0.11 mm (95% CI: −0.37 to 0.14, P=.39). Prosthetic complication relative risk was 1.26 (95% CI: 0.80–1.99, P=.31). Implant survival relative risk was 0.37% (95% CI: 0.10–1.36, P=.14). Both attachment categories performed equivalently across bone preservation, prosthetic maintenance, and implant retention.

## Key Contributions

1. **Splinting equivalence confirmed by meta-analysis**: No significant difference across MBL, complications, and survival — the clinical decision to splint or not may be driven by cost, patient preference, and hygiene access rather than clinical outcomes.
2. **PICO-focused design**: Unlike broader attachment-type reviews, this SR specifically isolated the splinting variable — methodologically cleaner for the splinted/unsplinted question.
3. **Quantified MBL difference is clinically negligible**: MD −0.11 mm between splinted and unsplinted is well within measurement error and far below the 1.5 mm first-year / 0.2 mm annual threshold for biological failure.
4. **Updates Stoumpis & Kohal (2011)**: Earlier qualitative SR on splinting upgraded to a quantitative SR+MA, strengthening the equivalence conclusion.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis (PRISMA)
- **PICO question**: Splinted vs unsplinted overdenture attachment systems — marginal bone loss, prosthetic complications, implant survival
- **Databases**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Scopus, Cochrane (up to October 2017)
- **Inclusion**: RCTs and prospective studies; ≥10 participants; ≥6-month follow-up; head-to-head splinted vs unsplinted comparison; English language
- **Studies included**: 9 articles
- **Sample**: 984 implants in 380 patients; mean age 62.8 years
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects; 95% CI; MD for continuous (MBL), RR for dichotomous (complications, survival)

### Clinical Summary

- Splinted and unsplinted systems achieve **equivalent** bone preservation, prosthetic durability, and implant survival.
- Neither system shows a biologically or clinically meaningful advantage over the other at the SR+MA level.
- Choice between bar (splinted) and independent attachments (unsplinted) can therefore be individualized based on: hygiene ability, cost, maintenance access, inter-implant angulation, and patient/clinician preference.
