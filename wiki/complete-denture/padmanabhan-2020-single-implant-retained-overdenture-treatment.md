## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 17 studies (11 in meta-analysis) finds single implant-retained mandibular overdentures achieve 5-year implant survival of 91.93% and 10-year survival of 84.62%; delayed loading is optimal and locator abutments require the most maintenance.

## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated treatment protocols for single implant-retained mandibular overdentures (SIRO). After searching 6 databases and screening 2,083 titles, 17 studies were included in the systematic review and 11 in the meta-analysis. A Poisson regression model was applied to estimate survival rates. Results showed high implant survival (91.93% at 5 years, 84.62% at 10 years), with delayed loading achieving the highest survival. Immediate loading outperformed early loading at both time points, though differences were not statistically significant. Ball abutments had higher fracture rates, while locator abutments required more frequent maintenance. The authors conclude that SIRO is a cost-effective, minimally invasive option for completely edentulous mandibular patients.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies implant survival for SIRO using Poisson regression: 91.93% (5-year) and 84.62% (10-year)
- Establishes delayed loading as the protocol with highest implant survival
- Demonstrates immediate loading is superior to early loading in SIRO (non-significant difference)
- Identifies differential complication profiles: ball abutments fracture more; locator abutments require more maintenance replacements
- Provides evidence base for SIRO as a simpler, cost-effective alternative to 2-implant overdentures

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **PICO**: Which surgical/prosthetic protocols yield highest survival/complication rates for SIRO in edentulous mandible?
- **Databases**: PubMed, ScienceDirect, COCHRANE, LILACS, IndeMED, OVID, EMBASE, NIH Clinical Trials
- **Inclusion**: English-language; minimum 1-year follow-up; single implant-retained overdentures
- **Screened**: 2,083 titles/abstracts → 17 included for SR; 11 for MA
- **Statistical method**: Poisson regression to model event rates per 100 prosthesis years
- **Risk of bias**: ROBINS-I (non-randomized) and RoB 2 (randomized trials)

## Results

| Outcome | Value |
|---|---|
| 5-year implant survival | 91.93% |
| 10-year implant survival | 84.62% |
| Best loading protocol (survival) | Delayed loading |
| Immediate vs. early loading at 5y | Immediate higher (p = 0.849, NS) |
| Immediate vs. early loading at 10y | Immediate higher (p = 0.464, NS) |
| Ball abutment fracture rate | 10.8 per 100 prosthesis years (95% CI: 10.5–11.09) |
| Locator abutment maintenance rate | 16.84 per 100 prosthesis years (95% CI: 16.01–17.66) |

**Loading protocol hierarchy (survival)**: Delayed > Immediate > Early (differences not statistically significant)

**Attachment complications**: Ball abutments fracture more; locator abutments require more frequent nylon insert replacement. Both are clinically manageable.

**Overall**: SIRO is a valid, cost-effective, minimally invasive treatment for edentulous mandibular patients, particularly those with financial constraints or systemic health limitations that make multi-implant surgery inadvisable.
