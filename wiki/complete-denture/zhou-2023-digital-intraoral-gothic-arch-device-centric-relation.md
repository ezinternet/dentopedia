## Summary

In complete-denture prosthodontics, the horizontal jaw relation — **centric relation (CR)** — is classically registered with a **Gothic arch (arrow-point) tracing**. The conventional intraoral tracer is accurate but adds chair time and requires careful set-up. This prospective, within-patient study (n=10 edentulous outpatients) asked whether a **digitally manufactured (CAD/CAM) simplified intraoral Gothic arch device** could replace the conventional intraoral device without losing accuracy.

Each patient had CR determined by **both** methods: the digital simplified tracer (T1) and the conventional intraoral tracer (T2). The two CR records were compared by 3D best-fit superimposition of the scanned casts and by clinical try-in. The digital device reproduced the conventional CR position to within **0.5 mm** in both the midline and molar regions (vertical and horizontal), and reduced clinical operating time significantly. The authors conclude the digital simplified intraoral Gothic arch device is a clinically acceptable, faster alternative for CR determination in complete dentures.

## Key Contributions

- First-described **CAD/CAM simplified intraoral Gothic arch tracer** validated against the conventional intraoral tracer for complete-denture CR.
- Sub-0.5 mm mandibular positional agreement (midline + molar regions) by STL best-fit superimposition.
- Significant chair-time reduction (~15.6 min) without measurable loss of CR accuracy.

## Methodology

- **Design:** prospective, within-patient paired comparison; n=10 edentulous outpatients.
- **Interventions:** T1 = digitally manufactured simple intraoral Gothic arch device; T2 = conventional intraoral Gothic arch device. Each patient received both.
- **Outcomes:**
  1. **Clinical operating time** for CR determination (minutes), paired test.
  2. **Positional accuracy** — maxillary/mandibular CR casts scanned → STL → Geomagic Studio; maxillary cast aligned by multi-point complete best-fit, then maximum mandibular positional deviation (T1 vs T2) measured in the **midline** and **molar** regions, vertical and labial(buccal)-lingual directions.
  3. **Clinical try-in** — denture midline deviation and posterior occlusal stability (tipping/rocking).

## Results

- **Chair time:** T1 (41.90 ± 2.64) min vs T2 (57.50 ± 2.37) min; paired test **P < 0.01**.
- **Mandibular deviation T1 vs T2:**

| Region | Labial/buccal–lingual | Vertical |
|---|---|---|
| Midline | 0.32 ± 0.14 mm | 0.40 ± 0.23 mm |
| Molar | 0.35 ± 0.23 mm | 0.33 ± 0.20 mm |

- All deviations (vertical and horizontal) **within 0.5 mm**.
- **Try-in:** neither group showed denture midline deviation or instability (no tipping/rocking).
- **Bottom line:** the digital simplified intraoral Gothic arch device is clinically usable for CR determination — saving time while preserving accuracy.
