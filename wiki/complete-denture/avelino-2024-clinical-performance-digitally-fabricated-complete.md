## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 16 studies found digitally fabricated complete dentures (milled or 3D-printed) have similar or better clinical performance vs conventional dentures, with no significant difference in patient satisfaction or OHRQoL.

## Summary

This 2024 systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO: CRD42023433909) from the Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry evaluated whether digitally fabricated complete dentures (CDs) — encompassing both CAD-CAM milled and 3D-printed techniques — improve clinical performance and patient-related outcome measures (PROMs) compared to conventionally fabricated CDs. A total of 1505 articles were screened and 16 included. 

Key meta-analytic findings: conventional CDs showed statistically better retention than 3D-printed CDs (P=.02), but no significant retention difference was found vs milled CDs (P=.20). For patient satisfaction, neither printed (P=.55) nor milled (P=.08) CDs showed significant differences from conventional CDs. All other clinical and PROM outcomes showed comparable or better results for digital CDs. The overall conclusion was that digitally fabricated CDs are clinically equivalent to or better than conventional CDs, and the manufacturing method did not meaningfully influence patient-reported outcomes.

## Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive clinical + PROM coverage**: Simultaneously synthesized 5 clinical outcomes (retention, adaptation/fit, clinician assessment, masticatory efficiency, occlusal force) and 4 PROMs (satisfaction, OHRQoL, final choice, WTP)
2. **Subgroup by fabrication method**: Separated analysis for 3D-printed vs milled CDs vs conventional, revealing that retention disadvantage is specific to printed CDs (not milled)
3. **GRADE evidence grading**: Applied rigorous certainty of evidence framework
4. **Dual risk-of-bias assessment**: Used both RoB 2 (RCTs) and ROBINS-I (non-randomized studies)
5. **Patient-centered focus**: Explicit PROM analysis including willingness-to-pay and final patient choice

## Methodology

- **Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis; PRISMA 2020 compliant
- **Registration**: PROSPERO CRD42023433909
- **Search**: Electronic databases up to September 2023; 1505 articles screened
- **Included studies**: 16 studies (mix of RCTs and non-randomized controlled studies)
- **Population**: Complete edentulous patients requiring or wearing CDs
- **Intervention**: Digitally fabricated CDs (milled CAD-CAM or 3D-printed)
- **Comparison**: Conventionally fabricated CDs
- **Statistical method**: Mean difference (MD) with 95% CI for continuous outcomes
- **Risk of bias**: RoB 2 (randomized) + ROBINS-I (non-randomized)
- **Evidence quality**: GRADE framework

## Results

### Clinical Performance — Retention (Meta-analysis)
| Comparison | Result | P-value |
|---|---|---|
| Conventional vs 3D-Printed CDs | Conventional significantly better | P=.02 |
| Conventional vs Milled CDs | No significant difference | P=.20 |

### PROMs — Patient Satisfaction (Meta-analysis)
| Comparison | Result | P-value |
|---|---|---|
| 3D-Printed vs Conventional | No significant difference | P=.55 |
| Milled vs Conventional | No significant difference | P=.08 |

### Other Outcomes (Qualitative synthesis)
- **Adaptation/fit**: Digital CDs comparable or better than conventional
- **Clinician's denture quality assessment**: Comparable
- **Masticatory efficiency**: Comparable between digital and conventional
- **Occlusal force**: Comparable
- **OHRQoL**: No meaningful difference between manufacturing methods
- **Final choice / willingness-to-pay**: No strong patient preference for either method

### Clinical Bottom Line
Digital complete dentures (both milled and printed) are clinically acceptable alternatives to conventional CDs. Printed CDs may have slightly lower retention than conventional, but this does not translate into lower patient satisfaction. Manufacturing method does not influence PROMs.
