## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 25 RCTs (2154 implants, 737 overdentures) found implant and overdenture survival is high regardless of attachment type, with LOCATOR showing fewest prosthetic complications and magnets having 7.4× more than other systems.

## Summary

This 2024 SR+MA from the Journal of Prosthetic Dentistry analyzed 25 RCTs to compare attachment systems for mandibular implant-supported overdentures. A total of 2154 implants and 737 overdentures were included. The overall implant failure rate was 2.0% (95% CI 1.3–3.2%) and overdenture failure rate was 4.2% (95% CI 1.6–10.5%), demonstrating high survival rates across all attachment types. No significant difference in biological complications was found between splinted and unsplinted overdentures (P=.902). For prosthetic complications, LOCATOR attachments performed best, followed by telescopic/Conus, bar, and ball attachments. Magnet attachments had 7.4× higher prosthetic complications than other attachment systems.

## Key Contributions

1. **RCT-exclusive evidence**: SR+MA restricted to RCTs only — highest level of evidence for clinical decision-making on attachment selection.
2. **Prosthetic complication hierarchy established**: LOCATOR < telescopic/Conus < bar < ball < magnet — actionable ranking for clinical choice.
3. **Splinting equivalence confirmed**: No significant biological complication difference between splinted (bar) and unsplinted attachments (P=.902), removing a key rationale for mandatory bar use.
4. **Magnet complication burden quantified**: 7.4× higher prosthetic complications — stronger evidence against routine magnet use than prior narrative reviews.
5. **Overall high survival established**: Both implant (98%) and prosthesis (95.8%) survival rates are robust regardless of attachment system choice.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **PROSPERO registration**: CRD42021253566
- **Databases**: PubMed, Cochrane, Embase, Scopus
- **Inclusion**: RCTs reporting implant and/or overdenture survival and/or complications for mandibular implant-supported overdentures
- **Total included**: 25 RCTs (from initial 477 screened)
- **RoB assessment**: Cochrane RoB 2 tool
- **Reporting**: PRISMA-compliant
- **Statistical analysis**: Pooled failure rates with 95% confidence intervals; association analysis between attachment type and outcomes; random-effects model

## Results

### Overall Survival Rates
| Outcome | Failure Rate | 95% CI |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Implant | 2.0% | 1.3–3.2% |
| Overdenture | 4.2% | 1.6–10.5% |

### Attachment-Specific Implant Failure (overlapping CIs — no significant differences)
| Attachment | Failure Rate | 95% CI |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| Bar | 7.7% | 3.0–18.1% |
| Magnet | 7.6% | 2.2–22.7% |
| Ball | 6.8% | 3.0–14.3% |

### Prosthetic Complications
- **Best**: LOCATOR (lowest rate)
- **Worst**: Magnet — **7.4× higher** prosthetic complications vs. all other attachments
- Order (fewest to most): LOCATOR < telescopic/Conus < bar < ball < magnet

### Biological Complications
- Splinted vs. unsplinted: P=.902 — **no significant difference**
- Marginal bone loss patterns similar across attachment types

### Clinical Takeaway
- Any attachment system achieves high implant and overdenture survival
- LOCATOR is the preferred unsplinted option based on lowest prosthetic complication burden
- Magnets should be used with caution due to substantially higher prosthetic maintenance burden
- Bar (splinted) confers no biological complication advantage over unsplinted systems
