## Summary

This 2025 expert consensus statement, published in the International Journal of Oral Science by a 27-author Chinese multi-institutional prosthodontics panel, addresses the lack of a unified clinical standard for digital complete denture (CD) restoration. It provides structured, practical recommendations spanning case indications, digital data acquisition (intraoral and facial scanning), custom tray complex design, jaw-relation recording and transfer, CAD tooth arrangement, additive vs subtractive manufacturing, follow-up/maintenance, and evaluation metrics for accuracy, performance, and patient satisfaction.

The consensus frames digital denture technology as offering clear efficiency and precision advantages over conventional fabrication — fewer visits, less chairside time (58–233 minutes saved), and comparable or superior retention when subtractive (milled) methods are used — while candidly flagging persistent gaps: intraoral scanning still struggles in movable-mucosa areas critical for border seal, denture stability lacks standardized objective evaluation, and no study has validated the superiority of any one jaw-relation-recording device (facebow, gothic arch tracer, articulator) within digital workflows specifically.

## Key Contributions

1. **Unified digital CD workflow protocol**: the first consensus-level standardization spanning case selection → digital acquisition → design → manufacture → follow-up, addressing inconsistencies clinicians face in workflow implementation and outcome evaluation.
2. **Scanning-method decision criteria**: explicit guidance on choosing intraoral vs extraoral (facial) scanning based on gag reflex, mouth opening, soft-tissue mobility, undercuts, and saliva control.
3. **Custom tray complex design detail**: tray body, detachable/non-detachable tissue stops, occlusion rims, and personalized gothic arch tracers, with a "mortise and tenon" adjustable tissue-stop design.
4. **Three jaw-relation transfer methods**: digital transfer to (1) average-value articulator, (2) personalized virtual articulator via facial scanning, (3) personalized virtual articulator via digital facebow/jaw-motion-tracking data.
5. **Manufacturing comparison**: additive (SLA, DLP, material jetting, powder bed fusion) vs subtractive (CNC milling, 3–5+ axis), including practical detail (wet vs dry milling, PMMA disc workflow).
6. **Evidence synthesis on outcomes**: retention force, occlusal force, masticatory efficiency, patient satisfaction/OHRQoL, and time/cost — reconciling mixed findings across the underlying literature.
7. **Special-scenario protocols**: RPD-to-CD transition workflow, and CD re-fabrication using the existing denture as the scanning/reference template.

## Methodology

As a consensus statement (not a primary study), the "methodology" is the consensus-development scope and process: collaborative synthesis by prosthodontic specialists and clinical experts across multiple institutions in China, drawing on current clinical evidence (including a cited systematic review of 39 studies) and collective expert judgment, structured into four practical domains — indications/case selection, clinical procedure/workflow, evaluation metrics, and outcome assessment.

Workflow domains addressed:
- **Digital data acquisition**: intraoral scanning (digital edentulous models), facial scanning (full-smiling and postural positions, facebow-fork registration, STL/OBJ/PLY export)
- **Custom tray complex**: digital design and 3D printing (light-cured resin, polylactic acid) of tray body, tissue stops, occlusion rims, gothic arch tracers
- **Definitive impression & jaw relation recording**: general method (post-dam spherical bulge for retrusion), gothic arch tracer method, jaw motion tracking system (ultrasonic/optical)
- **Digital design**: denture base, esthetic tooth arrangement (referencing tooth databases, facial profile/age/skin tone), occlusal design (bilateral balanced occlusion; virtual articulator)
- **Manufacture**: additive (SLA/DLP/material jetting/powder bed fusion) vs subtractive (CNC milling of PMMA discs, 3–5+ axis machines, wet vs dry milling)
- **Follow-up**: short-term post-insertion checks; long-term recall every 6–12 months
- **Special scenarios**: RPD-to-CD transition; CD re-fabrication from an existing denture

## Results

Key recommendations and evidence synthesis (not original clinical results):

- **Scanning accuracy**: intraoral scanning deviations within 0.2 mm of conventional impressions and <0.9 mm vs gypsum models — clinically acceptable, but a 39-study systematic review confirmed traditional border molding remains predominant for denture base fabrication because direct scanning underperforms in movable-mucosa regions (soft palate, sublingual area, vestibular sulcus) critical to border seal and retention.
- **Retention**: digital dentures measured 13–20 N; border-molded maxillary dentures up to 74 N; meta-analysis found conventional > 3D-printed retention, but milled dentures performed comparably to conventional; subtractive manufacturing outperforms injection molding (less polymerization shrinkage).
- **Manufacturing precision**: subtractive (milled) fabrication consistently more precise than additive across in vitro comparisons (lower SD in a 7-system comparison).
- **Occlusal/masticatory performance**: maximum occlusal force for digital CDs ranged 130–225 N; masticatory efficiency findings were mixed (some studies favor digital, others show no difference vs conventional).
- **Patient satisfaction/OHRQoL**: meta-analysis showed no significant difference between digital and conventional CDs, nor among conventional/milled/3D-printed subtypes; complication rates (pain/tenderness) also similar.
- **Esthetics**: some patients rate 3D-printed dentures less esthetically pleasing than milled (material uniformity favors milled); expected to improve with technology advances.
- **Efficiency/cost**: digital workflow saves 58–233 minutes of clinical time, with fabrication time roughly one-third of conventional methods and fewer required patient visits; high upfront equipment/training cost is a stated offsetting factor.
- **Recall interval recommendation**: 6–12 months for comprehensive denture/base/occlusion/mucosal health assessment, with relining or remaking as indicated.
- **Future direction flagged by the panel**: AI and augmented reality (AR) integration into CAD/CAM workflows to improve scanning accuracy and enable individualized esthetic customization.
