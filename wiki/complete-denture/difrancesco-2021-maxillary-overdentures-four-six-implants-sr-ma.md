## One-line Summary
SR+MA (15 comparative studies, 1–10 yr follow-up): for splinted bar-supported maxillary overdentures, four versus six implants show no significant difference in implant survival (p=0.34), overdenture survival (p=0.74), prosthodontic complications, or patient satisfaction across both random-effects and Fisher's-test analyses — four splinted implants are a viable, more conservative choice.

## Summary
Maxillary overdentures (OD) are more demanding than mandibular OD: bone quality is lower, ridge resorption greater, and biomechanical load distribution harder. A common clinical question is whether **six implants** are necessary or whether **four splinted implants** are sufficient. This 2021 PRISMA systematic review and meta-analysis from BMC Oral Health screened 1865 articles, retained 15 comparative studies (1–10 yr follow-up, ≥10 patients), and applied both random-effects meta-analysis and non-parametric Fisher's tests for robustness.

The result is consistent across both statistical approaches: **no significant difference in implant survival, overdenture survival, prosthodontic complications, or patient satisfaction between 4 vs 6 splinted implants**. Patient satisfaction was uniformly high across all included studies regardless of implant count. The authors conclude that bar-supported maxillary OD on four splinted implants is a viable, more conservative option than the traditional six-implant design.

## Key Contributions
- First SR+MA dedicated to the 4 vs 6 splinted-implant maxillary OD question.
- Shows the answer is robust across two statistical methods (random-effects MA + Fisher's test).
- Provides cost-effective guidance: fewer implants, comparable outcome.

## Methodology
- Databases: Cochrane Central, MEDLINE (PubMed), EMBASE; hand search 2000–2019.
- Inclusion: 4 vs 6 implants supporting maxillary OD, ≥1-yr follow-up, ≥10 fully edentulous patients.
- 15 of 1865 articles retained.
- RoB: Cochrane RoB 2 + Newcastle-Ottawa; randomized studies often high RoB, non-randomized acceptable.

## Results
| Outcome | 4 vs 6 implants — p value |
|---|---|
| Implant survival | p=0.34 (meta), p=0.3 (Fisher) — NS |
| Overdenture survival | p=0.74 (meta), p=0.9 (Fisher) — NS |
| Prosthodontic complications | No significant difference |
| Patient satisfaction | Uniformly high in both arms |

Clinical implication: when bone permits placement of four well-distributed implants splinted by a bar, **adding a fifth or sixth implant does not improve patient-meaningful outcomes**. The decision can default to 4 implants and reserve 6 for select biomechanical situations (long cantilevers, severely atrophic ridges, or parafunction).
