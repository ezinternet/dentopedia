## One-line Summary

SR+MA of 19 RCTs: simplified complete denture fabrication saves ~77 min chair time and ~$86/case cost with no significant difference in patient satisfaction or OHRQoL at 6 months vs. conventional technique.

## Summary

This SR+MA (Cochrane methodology) synthesizes 19 RCTs comparing **simplified** versus **conventional (traditional)** complete denture fabrication techniques in edentulous adults. Simplified techniques — typically reducing clinical appointments from 5–6 to 2–4, using stock-tray impressions, simplified jaw records without facebow, and fewer try-in steps — were compared against conventional multi-stage workflows.

Key pooled findings: patient satisfaction (SMD = 0.02, 95% CI: −0.22 to 0.27) showed no clinically or statistically significant difference. Simplified techniques saved a mean of **77.34 minutes of clinical time** (MD = −77.34, 95% CI: −122.45 to −32.24) and **$85.89 in fabrication cost** (MD = −85.89, 95% CI: −170.02 to −1.77). High risk of bias and heterogeneity across studies limit confidence, but the directional consistency across outcomes supports the efficiency of simplified approaches.

## Key Contributions

1. **Quantified time savings**: ~77 minutes saved per case — a clinically meaningful reduction for high-volume practices and resource-limited settings.
2. **Quantified cost savings**: ~$86 USD saved per case — supports appropriateness for public dental health services and low-income populations.
3. **Equivalent patient satisfaction**: No significant difference in OHRQoL (OHIP-EDENT, OHIP-14) or VAS satisfaction scores at 6 months.
4. **RCT-only evidence base**: Restricting inclusion to RCTs provides the highest-quality available evidence, though most trials carry high risk of bias.
5. **Heterogeneity flag**: Diverse operationalization of "simplified" across studies is the primary barrier to stronger conclusions; standardization is needed.

## Methodology

- **Study design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis (Cochrane methodology)
- **Search**: MEDLINE/PubMed, CENTRAL, Scopus + manual search through February 2020
- **Included studies**: 19 RCTs; edentulous adults requiring complete dentures
- **Excluded**: Implant overdentures, partial dentures, digital/CAD-CAM trials
- **Statistical model**: Random-effects; SMD for patient satisfaction, MD for time and cost
- **Heterogeneity**: I² statistic; high heterogeneity noted
- **Risk of bias**: Cochrane RoB tool — most trials rated high risk (no blinding of participants/operators feasible)

**"Simplified technique" definition** (pooled): reduced appointments (2–4 vs. 5–6), stock tray + one-step impression, simplified centric relation record (no facebow/Gothic arch), and reduced or no wax try-in.

## Results

| Outcome | Comparison | MD/SMD | 95% CI | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Patient satisfaction | Simplified vs. Conventional | SMD = +0.02 | −0.22 to +0.27 | NS |
| Clinical time (min) | Simplified vs. Conventional | MD = −77.34 | −122.45 to −32.24 | Significant |
| Fabrication cost (USD) | Simplified vs. Conventional | MD = −85.89 | −170.02 to −1.77 | Significant |

- OHRQoL (OHIP-EDENT/OHIP-14): no significant difference at 6 months
- Masticatory performance: insufficient data for pooling
- Longest follow-up: 10 years (Kawai et al., 2018) — no difference in satisfaction maintained

**Clinical implication**: Simplified complete denture fabrication can be recommended for routine edentulous patient care, particularly where resource efficiency, access, or clinical time are priorities, without compromising patient-centered outcomes.
