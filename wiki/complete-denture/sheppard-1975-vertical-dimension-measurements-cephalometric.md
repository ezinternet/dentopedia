## One-line Summary

Classic cephalometric study (n=50 complete denture wearers) comparing facial-marker vs skeletal-landmark vertical-dimension measurements of mandibular rest position, edentulous vs with dentures: facial soft tissue masks true mandibular movement, the rest position is variable and shifts on denture insertion, so the edentulous rest position is not a reliable basis for determining OVD.

## Summary

Establishing occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) in edentulous patients classically relies on the mandibular rest position minus a freeway space. Sheppard & Sheppard tested how dependable that reference is. Using cephalometric radiographs with both facial lead markers and skeletal landmarks on 50 complete-denture wearers, they measured rest-position vertical dimension with and without dentures. Facial soft-tissue measurements masked skeletal mandibular movement; the edentulous rest position was variable and frequently changed (usually increased) when dentures were inserted, with the direction of change relating to denture age/experience. The practical conclusion — still cited — is that the edentulous rest position is a weak sole basis for OVD, especially when judged by facial measurements, motivating the use of multiple corroborating methods.

## Key Contributions

- Radiographic evidence that the edentulous mandibular rest position is unstable and a poor sole reference for OVD.
- Demonstrated that facial (soft-tissue) measurement masks true skeletal mandibular position.

## Methodology

- **Subjects**: 50 complete denture wearers.
- **Imaging**: cephalometric radiographs; vertical dimension of mandibular rest position measured between facial lead markers and between skeletal landmarks.
- **Conditions**: edentulous (no dentures) vs edentoprosthetic (dentures in mouth).

## Results

- Facial structures **masked** mandibular positional change.
- Edentulous rest position was **variable**; its measured range widened with greater denture age/experience.
- Edentoprosthetic rest position was **more constant skeletally** than facially, and more constant than the edentulous rest position.
- Denture insertion **increased** the rest position in most subjects (decreased in others); those who decreased had older dentures / more wearing experience.
- **Conclusion**: the edentulous mandibular rest position is **not particularly suitable** for determining VDO — least of all via facial measurements; corroborate with additional methods.
