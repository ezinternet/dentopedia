## Summary

This 2021 systematic review and meta-analysis (Journal of Dentistry) by Srinivasan et al. is the most comprehensive synthesis comparing CAD-CAM complete dentures (milled and 3D-printed) against conventionally fabricated complete dentures across multiple outcome domains. Seventy-three studies were included after a search through PubMed, Embase, and CENTRAL databases (final search 15 March 2021). Inter-rater reliability was excellent (kappa 0.897–1.000). Meta-analyses were performed across mechanical properties, surface characteristics, trueness of fit, color stability, retention, time-cost analysis, and patient-reported outcomes.

The overarching conclusion is that CAD-CAM complete dentures are not inferior to conventional dentures and offer several significant advantages, particularly in mechanical performance and efficiency. Milled CDs consistently outperform both conventional and 3D-printed CDs on most mechanical benchmarks. 3D-printed CDs show an advantage in trueness of fit but lag in esthetics, color stability, and mechanical strength compared to milled. CAD-CAM fabrication requires significantly less chairside time and costs less overall, with the added clinical advantage of preserving a digital record — particularly valuable for elderly patients with limited access to care.

## Key Contributions

1. **Comprehensive multi-domain SR+MA**: First study to synthesize trueness of fit, biocompatibility, mechanical properties, surface characteristics, color stability, time-cost, clinical outcomes, and PROs in a single review for CAD-CAM complete dentures.
2. **Milled CDs mechanically superior**: Flexural strength, flexural modulus, yield strength, and toughness all significantly higher for milled vs conventional and 3D-printed.
3. **3D-printed CDs more accurate**: Better trueness of fit than conventional (p = 0.039), though esthetics inferior (p < 0.0001).
4. **CAD-CAM retention advantage**: Pooled CAD-CAM CDs showed superior retention vs conventional (p = 0.015).
5. **Time-cost efficiency**: CAD-CAM requires less chairside time (p = 0.037) and lower overall cost (p < 0.0001).
6. **Digital record preservation**: Highlighted as a clinical advantage, especially for geriatric patients with limited access to follow-up care.

## Methodology

- **Study type**: Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Databases**: PubMed (MEDLINE), Embase, CENTRAL; supplemented by Google, ResearchGate, hand searches
- **Included studies**: 73 (all study designs — in vitro, in vivo, clinical)
- **Fabrication methods**: Conventional (heat-polymerized PMMA, injection-molded), milled CAD-CAM (subtractive, CNC), 3D-printed (additive: SLA, DLP, FDM, PolyJet/rapid prototyping)
- **Outcomes**: Trueness of fit (surface deviation in mm), biocompatibility, mechanical properties (flexural strength/modulus, yield strength, toughness, hardness, strain), surface properties (roughness, contact angle, staining), color stability, time-cost, retention, OHRQoL, satisfaction
- **Statistical analysis**: Random-effects meta-analyses; subgroup analyses by fabrication method
- **Inter-rater reliability**: Kappa 0.897–1.000

## Results

### Trueness of Fit
- 3D-printed > conventional (p = 0.039)
- Optimal 3D-print build orientation: 45° for mandibular dentures
- Post-polymerization: 30 min at 40°C optimal

### Mechanical Properties
| Property | Best performer | p value |
|---|---|---|
| Flexural strength | Milled > conventional | p < 0.0001 |
| Flexural strength | Milled > 3D-printed | p < 0.0001 |
| Flexural modulus | Milled > 3D-printed | p < 0.0001 |
| Yield strength | Milled > injection-molded | p = 0.004 |
| Yield strength | Milled > 3D-printed | p = 0.001 |
| Toughness | Milled superior | p < 0.0001 |
| Strain at yield | Conventional > milled | p < 0.0001 |

### Surface Characteristics
- Surface roughness: Milled > conventional and 3D-printed (p < 0.0001)
- Color stability: Rapidly-prototyped inferior to other types (p = 0.029)
- Esthetics: Conventional > 3D-printed (p < 0.0001)

### Clinical Outcomes
- Retention: CAD-CAM > conventional (p = 0.015)

### Time-Cost
- Chairside time: CAD-CAM requires less (p = 0.037)
- Overall cost: CAD-CAM lower (p < 0.0001)

### Patient-Reported Outcomes
- OHRQoL and satisfaction: CAD-CAM CDs not inferior to conventional in clinical studies
