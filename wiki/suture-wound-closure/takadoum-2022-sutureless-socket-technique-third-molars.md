## One-line Summary
Multicentric open RCT (3 French hospitals, n=94 analyzed: 44 suture / 50 without) of suture vs sutureless extraction of four impacted third molars under general anesthesia; no difference in Day-3 pain (p=0.904) or any secondary outcome, with shorter operating time sutureless and smoking a risk factor for complications (3.65× higher, p=0.0244).

## Summary
The largest and most rigorous trial in this cluster: a multicentric (Nîmes, Montpellier, Perpignan), CONSORT-compliant, open RCT in which patients having all four impacted wisdom teeth removed under general anesthesia were randomized 1:1 to suture vs sutureless socket closure. With follow-up extended to Day 31, the trial found no difference in the primary outcome (Day-3 pain, p=0.904) nor in any secondary outcome (edema, trismus, painkillers, healing, complications, quality of life). Operating time was shorter without sutures, and smoking independently raised the complication rate 3.65-fold. This establishes the equivalence baseline against which more positive single-center trials should be interpreted.

## Key Contributions
- Multicentric, adequately powered RCT with Day-31 follow-up (longer than the typical ≤7-day window).
- Demonstrates non-inferiority of sutureless closure on pain and all secondary outcomes, plus a practical operating-time saving.
- Quantifies smoking as a strong independent complication risk factor (3.65×, p=0.0244).

## Methodology
- Parallel 1:1, non-blinded RCT across 3 French university hospitals; electronic allocation; CONSORT-compliant (NCT02583997).
- Population: ≥14 y, extraction of all four impacted wisdom teeth under general anesthesia. Excluded: antiplatelet/anticoagulant use, coagulation disorders, immunosuppression, functional teeth, emergency pain/infection, pregnancy/breastfeeding.
- Standardized surgery; Suture group vicryl 4.0 (single, two stitches, or cross); Without-Suture group flap held by jaw pressure (sutured only if bleeding/poor repositioning).
- Primary outcome: Day-3 pain (0–10 VAS). Secondary: pain Day 0/31, edema (%), trismus (%), complications, flap healing, painkillers, GOHAI quality of life, operating time, smoking impact.
- Analysis adjusted for center, age, smoking.

## Results
- 100 randomized (Jun 2016–Nov 2018); 94 analyzed (44 suture, 50 without). Mean age 16.5 y, 66.6% female.
- Day-3 pain: no difference (p=0.904, adjusted).
- No differences in swelling, trismus, painkiller use, healing, complications, or quality of life.
- Smoking: 3.65× higher complication rate (p=0.0244).
- Operating time shorter without sutures.
- Conclusion: sutureless removal is reliable with no negative consequence on outcomes and shorter operating time; smoking is a complication risk factor.
