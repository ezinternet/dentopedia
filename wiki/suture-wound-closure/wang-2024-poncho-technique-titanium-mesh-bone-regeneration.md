## One-line Summary

Case series (5 patients, 23 surgical sites, severe atrophic ridges with customized titanium mesh) showing the modified "Poncho" technique (MPT) achieved uneventful soft-tissue healing at 95.7% of sites (1/23 partial dehiscence) with Landry's healing index rising from 3 ± 0.47 to 4.69 ± 1.06 over 30 days.

## Summary

Soft-tissue dehiscence and titanium-mesh exposure (documented at 15.2–23.9% in the literature) are the dominant complications of mesh-based GBR, especially over severely atrophic ridges where conventional advancement methods fall short. This case series introduces the modified "Poncho" technique (MPT) — a multilayer flap design with a defined double-suture closure — for tension-free passive primary closure when major flap elevation is required beneath a digitally customized titanium mesh. Across 5 patients (23 sites), 95.7% of sites healed uneventfully with only one partial dehiscence, and Landry's healing index improved steadily over 30 days. MPT is presented as a promising, predictable closure approach for major-advancement Ti-mesh cases.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a reproducible step-by-step MPT protocol for major flap advancement over customized Ti-mesh.
- Specifies a two-line double-suture closure strategy (deep submucosal-to-palatal mattress + superficial mucosal interrupted) to distribute tension.
- Reports early healing quantitatively via Landry's healing index at 3/7/14/30 days.

## Methodology

- Case series: 5 patients, 23 surgical sites, customized bone regeneration (CBR) with customized titanium mesh.
- Inclusion: atrophic ridges (horizontal/vertical/mixed), residual bone <6 mm height and <4 mm width, staged implant rehabilitation.
- MPT steps: superficial buccal horizontal incision beyond MGJ → buccal vertical + palatal sulcus extension → strategic buccal vertical-releasing incisions (papilla-preserving; ≥2 teeth beyond target, or ≥15 mm if edentulous) → oblique dissection through muscular-submucosal layer then periosteum with blunt separation (~10–20 mm) → palatal full-thickness flap elevation → GBR (graft + customized mesh + resorbable membrane) → double-suture closure (4-0 horizontal mattress deep + 5-0 interrupted superficial, polyglactin), cross-linked hyaluronic acid (xHyA) referenced.
- Outcomes: complications (dehiscence, mesh exposure, infection, necrosis) + Landry's healing index at 3, 7, 14, 30 days.

## Results

- 95.7% of sites healed uneventfully during observation.
- 1 of 23 sites showed partial dehiscence at days 14 and 30 with mild inflammation.
- Landry's healing index rose from 3 ± 0.47 to 4.69 ± 1.06.
- Conclusion: MPT shows promise for passive, predictable primary closure beneath customized Ti-mesh, with high healing success and minimal early complications.
