## One-line Summary

Case series (17 patients, anterior-maxilla lateral ridge augmentation) showing a 940-nm diode-laser periosteal releasing incision achieved tension-free primary closure in all cases with minimal bleeding, mild pain (VAS 20.6 ± 12.1 mm at day 1), no hematoma/intense swelling, and 100% implant success at 6 months post-loading.

## Summary

Periosteal releasing incision (PRI) is the standard maneuver for tension-free primary closure in GBR, but scalpel PRI can cause excessive bleeding (impairing field visualization and risking graft-particle displacement) and meaningful postoperative morbidity. This case series — described by the authors as the first clinical use of laser for flap advancement in bone augmentation — performed PRI with a 940-nm diode laser in 17 patients undergoing anterior-maxilla lateral ridge augmentation. All cases achieved tension-free primary closure with minimal bleeding, uneventful healing, no dehiscence/infection, only mild pain and swelling, and 100% implant success at 6 months post-loading.

## Key Contributions

- First reported clinical use (per authors) of a diode laser to perform periosteal releasing incision / flap advancement in GBR.
- Demonstrates low intraoperative bleeding and low early-postoperative morbidity (no intense swelling, mild pain, NSAID use ending by day 4).
- Shows no adverse effect on implant survival at 6 months post-loading.

## Methodology

- 17 healthy nonsmokers (8M/9F, 31–65 y) requiring anterior-maxilla lateral ridge augmentation; one defect each via GBR.
- Full-thickness mid-crestal incision + two buccal vertical releasing incisions; flap elevated to anterior nasal spine.
- 940-nm GaAlAs diode laser (ezLase, Biolase), 400-µm tip, contact mode, 2 W pulsed (1 ms interval / 1 ms length), aimed parallel to buccal bone to avoid bone irradiation.
- Tenting screws + DFDBA (Cenobone) + resorbable membrane (Cenoderm 2 mm); internal mattress + continuous mattress sutures.
- Postop: amoxicillin 500 mg TID ×7 d, NSAID, 0.2% chlorhexidine BID ×14 d.
- Outcomes: swelling (0–3) and pain (VAS 0–100 mm) daily ×6 d, NSAID count; implant success (Albrektsson) 6 months post-loading.

## Results

- Minimal bleeding; tension-free primary closure in all cases; uneventful healing; no dehiscence or infection.
- No intense swelling; peak swelling day 3 (1.47 ± 0.50), resolved by day 6.
- Pain peaked day 1: mean VAS 20.59 ± 12.10 mm (mild); resolved by day 6.
- NSAID: mean 1.53 ± 1.04 capsules day 1; none after day 4.
- 100% implant success/function at 6 months post-loading.
