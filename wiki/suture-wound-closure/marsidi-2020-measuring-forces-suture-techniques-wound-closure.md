## One-line Summary

Ex-vivo biomechanical bench study (5 suture techniques × 10 reps × 3 operators on a 5 N standardized neoprene wound model): the pulley suture required the least pulling force (3.46 N) to close a high-tension wound vs single (5.69 N), vertical mattress (7.25 N), and horizontal mattress (8.11 N, highest).

## Summary

Marsidi et al. quantified the pulling force needed to close a standardized 5 N high-tension wound (laser-cut neoprene substitute) using five suture techniques, measured with a validated ForceTRAP platform and Hook-in-Force strand sensor. Each of three operators (student, resident, dermsurgeon) performed each technique 10 times with 3-0 nylon. The **pulley suture** was the most mechanically efficient — closing the defect with only **3.46 N** of pulling force — while the **horizontal mattress required the most (8.11 N)**. Notably, the force actually transmitted to the skin substitute was similar across all techniques (0.80-0.96 N), so the large differences are in surgeon effort/strand tension rather than final edge tension. The clinical inference is that lower pulling force (pulley) may reduce the risk of overtightening and edge ischemia in high-tension closures.

## Key Contributions

- First quantitative ranking of suture techniques by **pulling force** in a standardized high-tension model.
- Pulley < modified pulley < single < vertical mattress < horizontal mattress, by required pulling force.
- Methodologic contribution: reproducible neoprene wound model + dual force sensors.

## Methodology

- Neoprene pad (9.5×9.5 cm, 3.5 mm), 2 cm elliptical defect, 30° edge angle, pretensioned to 5 N ± 0.1.
- ForceTRAP (0-20 N, 3D, 0.1 N accuracy) for skin-side tension; Hook-in-Force (0-15 N, 0.5 N accuracy) for strand pulling force.
- Ethilon 3-0 monofilament nylon; 5 techniques × 10 reps × 3 operators.
- Outcomes: mean maximum pulling force per technique; mean force increase on skin substitute.

## Results

| Technique | Mean max pulling force (N ± SD) |
|---|---|
| Pulley | 3.46 ± 0.61 |
| Modified pulley | 4.52 ± 0.67 |
| Single interrupted | 5.69 ± 0.88 |
| Vertical mattress | 7.25 ± 1.33 |
| Horizontal mattress | 8.11 ± 1.00 |

- Force transmitted to skin substitute: 0.80 N (pulley) to 0.96 N (vertical mattress).
- **Limitation (living-document note):** neoprene substitute only; no tissue creep, vascular, or healing endpoints. First-throw pulling force only. Inference about ischemia is mechanical, not clinically demonstrated.
