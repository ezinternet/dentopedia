## One-line Summary

Systematic review (PubMed + manual search, English articles Jan 1990–Sep 2015) categorizing GBR flap designs by required advancement — minor (<3 mm), moderate (3–6 mm), major (≥7 mm) — and the incision/release/suturing techniques that achieve passive primary closure under the PASS principle.

## 한줄요약

GBR 피판 디자인을 필요한 전진량(소: <3mm, 중: 3–6mm, 대: ≥7mm)으로 분류하고 무장력 일차 봉합을 위한 절개·이완·봉합 기법을 PASS 원칙에 따라 정리한 체계적 문헌고찰(1990–2015).

## Summary

This systematic/comprehensive review provides the organizing framework for the whole flap-advancement collection: it classifies GBR flap designs by the amount of advancement needed to achieve passive primary closure — minor (<3 mm), moderate (3–6 mm), major (≥7 mm) — following Greenstein et al. (2009), and maps incision design, reflection, releasing, and suturing techniques onto each stratum. Success is framed around the PASS principle (Primary closure, Angiogenesis, Stability, Space maintenance). The review quantifies how much each maneuver contributes to advancement and documents the dominant complication (membrane exposure) and its regenerative cost, motivating the technique-specific papers in this category.

## Key Contributions

- A practical decision framework: choose flap-advancement technique by required advancement stratum.
- Quantified advancement contributions (per Park et al.): first vertical incision +1.1 mm, second +1.9 mm, periosteal releasing incision +5.5 mm from baseline.
- Consolidates the PASS principle and the exposure-vs-regeneration relationship as the rationale for tension-free closure.

## Methodology

- Two reviewers searched PubMed plus manual journal searches (IJOMI, COIR, Implant Dentistry, J Periodontol, IJPRD).
- Search terms: "flap designs," "guided bone regeneration," "ridge augmentation," "socket augmentation," and pairs.
- Inclusion: English articles January 1990 – September 2015.
- Designs grouped by flap-extension capacity per Greenstein et al. (2009). Narrative synthesis (no meta-analysis).

## Results

- Advancement strata and techniques:
  - Minor (<3 mm): full-thickness reflection beyond the MGJ (e.g., esthetic buccal flap, mucogingival pouch flap).
  - Moderate (3–6 mm): two vertical releasing incisions + ~1 mm periosteal scoring as needed.
  - Major (≥7 mm): deeper periosteal scoring (3–5 mm into submucosa); split-thickness flaps for added coverage.
- Complication context: membrane exposure is the most common GBR complication; vertical GBR complication rate 0–45.5% vs horizontal 0–24%; exposure range 6.95–13.1%; ePTFE exposure dropped regeneration 96.6%→46.5%; ~6× more bone gain in non-exposed vs exposed sites; immediate implants higher wound opening (80%) than delayed (17–32%).
