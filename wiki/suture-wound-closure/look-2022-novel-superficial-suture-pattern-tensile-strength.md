## Summary

Look et al. performed the first tensile-strength comparison of suture patterns on **human cadaveric skin**, testing simple (S), horizontal-mattress (HM), vertical-mattress (VM), and a novel **Lindeque locking (LL)** pattern (12 samples each, 3-0 nylon, 1 N/s linear loading). The LL stitch produced the **highest dehiscence force (198.60 N)** and **ultimate load-to-failure (211.13 N)**, but its statistical superiority held only against the HM pattern (p<0.001 for both), which was significantly the weakest (104.81 N dehiscence; 120.79 N failure). Against the simple stitch there was no significant difference on either outcome, and against VM the LL edge was significant only for ultimate load, not dehiscence. The practical takeaways: (1) the horizontal mattress is the weakest of the common patterns for resisting separation, and (2) the LL stitch may help in high-tension wounds, though its advantage over simple/vertical-mattress is partial.

## Key Contributions

- First human-cadaveric-skin tensile-strength comparison of suture patterns.
- Introduces the Lindeque locking (LL) superficial pattern; highest absolute strength.
- Flags the horizontal mattress as mechanically weakest for resisting dehiscence/failure.

## Methodology

- 4 patterns (S, HM, VM, LL) × 12 samples each on human cadaveric skin; 3-0 nylon.
- Tensioning device: 1 N/s linear loading.
- Outcomes: wound dehiscence force; ultimate load-to-failure.
- One-way ANOVA + post-hoc Tukey.

## Results

| Pattern | Dehiscence force (N) | Ultimate load-to-failure (N) |
|---|---|---|
| Lindeque locking (LL) | 198.60 | 211.13 |
| Simple (S) | 186.90 | 195.08 |
| Vertical-mattress (VM) | 173.9 | 171.1 |
| Horizontal-mattress (HM) | 104.81 | 120.79 |

- LL significantly > HM on both (dehiscence 95% CI 65.7-121.9, p<0.001; failure 95% CI 63.5-117.2, p<0.001).
- LL vs S: no significant difference (both outcomes).
- LL vs VM: significant for ultimate load only, not dehiscence.
- **Living-document note:** cadaveric mechanical endpoints only (no healing/perfusion); n=12/group; single suture material; LL benefit robust only vs HM.
