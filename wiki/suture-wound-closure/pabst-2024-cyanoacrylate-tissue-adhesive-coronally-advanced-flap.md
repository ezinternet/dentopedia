Ex-vivo biomechanical study (36 half-pig mandibles, n=12 per group): cyanoacrylate tissue adhesive (TA) gave significantly higher CAF marginal flap stability than sutures alone (5.20 vs 0.88 N maximum tensile force), and TA + sutures combined was highest (8.50 N; all p<0.001).

## Summary

Pabst et al. tested whether cyanoacrylate-based tissue adhesive (TA; PeriAcryl 90 HV) improves coronally advanced flap (CAF) marginal stability versus conventional suturing in an ex-vivo half-pig-mandible model (36 mandibles, 3 groups × 12). A 3-mm recession was created at a mandibular premolar, a CAF prepared by the split-full-split technique and advanced 1 mm above the CEJ, then fixed by (I) sling-and-tag sutures, (II) three layers of TA, or (III) both. An Instron machine pulled the flap apically (0.5 mm/s) until the marked CEJ reappeared, recording maximum tensile force. TA alone (**5.20 N**) hugely outperformed sutures alone (**0.88 N**), and TA + sutures was highest (**8.50 N**), all differences p<0.001. The mechanistic interpretation is that polymerized cyanoacrylate bonds areally over the whole flap, whereas sutures fix only at points. Keratinized-tissue width and gingival thickness did not correlate with tensile force. The clinical relevance is that CTA may be a valuable adjunct to suturing in periodontal plastic surgery where high flap stability is needed — but this is an immediate mechanical proxy in pig tissue, not a healing/coverage outcome.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies a large flap-stability advantage of cyanoacrylate TA over sutures alone in CAF, with an additive benefit when combined.
- Suggests areal adhesive bonding > punctual suture fixation for marginal flap adaptation.

## Methodology

- 36 half-pig mandibles, 3 groups (n=12): (I) sling-and-tag sutures, (II) TA ×3 layers, (III) sutures + TA.
- CAF per de Sanctis & Zucchelli split-full-split; advanced 1 mm above CEJ; 3-mm recession at P3.
- TA: PeriAcryl 90 HV (n-butyl + 2-octyl cyanoacrylate).
- Instron 5942, pull-off at 0.5 mm/s parallel to tooth axis until CEJ visible; max tensile force.
- Mann-Whitney U; linear regression KT/GT vs force.

## Results

| Group | Max tensile force (N ± SD) |
|---|---|
| I — Sutures alone | 0.88 ± 0.61 |
| II — Tissue adhesive | 5.20 ± 1.47 |
| III — Sutures + TA | 8.50 ± 2.30 |

- II > I, III > I, III > II all p<0.001.
- KT/GT not correlated with tensile force (p=0.19, p=0.46).
- **Living-document note:** ex-vivo pig model; immediate mechanical pull-off only; no healing/recession-coverage data. Authors explicitly state limited clinical transferability and call for long-term follow-up. Single TA product, single suture technique.
