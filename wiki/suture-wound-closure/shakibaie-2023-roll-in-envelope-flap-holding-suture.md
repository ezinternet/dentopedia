## One-line Summary

Prospective case series (10 posterior implants, 10 patients, randomly assigned by flap design) reporting that the microsurgical roll-in-envelope (RIE) flap yielded a ~three-fold greater peri-implant mucosal thickness than the holding-suture (HS) technique with less pain/discomfort and no dropouts.

## Summary

This prospective case series addresses peri-implant soft-tissue management rather than barrier/mesh coverage: it compares a microsurgical roll-in-envelope (RIE) flap against the more commonly used holding-suture (HS) technique for thickening the peri-implant mucosa. Ten posterior implants in ten healthy patients were randomly assigned by flap design, with mucosal thickness measured at surgery and at 6 and 12 weeks. Healing was uneventful with no dropouts, and the HS group showed roughly a three-fold reduction in mucosal thickness relative to RIE. The authors conclude that, when peri-implant supporting tissue is sufficient and the case is indicated, the RIE flap can yield superior outcomes while reducing pain/discomfort compared with connective tissue grafts (CTG).

## Key Contributions

- Describes the microsurgical RIE flap as a minimally invasive soft-tissue-thickening alternative to CTG.
- Provides a head-to-head (randomized within a case series) comparison of RIE vs HS on peri-implant mucosal thickness.
- Frames RIE as a lower-morbidity option when sufficient supporting tissue exists.

## Methodology

- 10 posterior dental implants in 10 healthy individuals.
- Randomly assigned to RIE vs HS by flap design.
- Main outcome: mucosal thickness at time of surgery and at 6 and 12 weeks.
- RIE performed microsurgically (operating microscope).

## Results

- Uneventful healing at all sites; no patient drop-outs.
- ~Three-fold reduction in mucosal thickness in HS vs RIE (RIE preserved/created the greater thickness).
- Conclusion: with sufficient supporting tissue and proper indication, RIE yields superior outcomes and reduces pain/discomfort vs CTG.
- Exact thickness values, SDs, and p-values not present in the available text.
