## One-line Summary

Three-month parallel-design RCT (60 enrolled, 52 completed; 4 groups of n=13): in free gingival graft surgery, graft technique mattered more than suture technique — gingival unit transfer (GUT) showed less graft shrinkage than conventional graft (CG) regardless of suture, while the modified sling suture (MSS) gave no advantage and GUT+conventional-suture had the largest keratinized-tissue-width gain.

## 한줄요약

3개월 평행설계 RCT (60명 등록, 52명 완료; 4군 각 n=13): 유리치은이식술(FGG)에서 봉합법보다 이식편 기법이 더 중요했음 — 치은단위이식(GUT)이 봉합법과 무관하게 통상이식(CG)보다 이식편 수축이 적었고, 변형 슬링봉합(MSS)은 이점이 없었으며 GUT+통상봉합이 각화조직폭 증가가 가장 컸음.

## Summary

This RCT (NCT06197893) crossed two factors in free gingival graft (FGG) surgery — suture technique (modified sling suture MSS vs conventional suture CS) and graft technique (gingival unit transfer GUT vs conventional graft CG) — across four groups (n=13), in patients with Cairo RT2/RT3 mandibular-anterior recessions. The pre-registered hypothesis was that the less-invasive MSS (a sling variant incorporating the lingual papilla) would reduce graft shrinkage. It did not. The signal came instead from the graft technique: **GUT showed less graft surface-area shrinkage than CG regardless of suture**, and the worst shrinkage was in **CG+MSS** (greater than both GUT groups, p<0.05). For keratinized-tissue-width gain, **GUT+CS** was significantly higher than GUT+MSS. Recession height, vestibule depth, and graft shrinkage were otherwise statistically similar across groups, and baseline measurements did not correlate with shrinkage. Bottom line for the clinic: choice of graft harvest (GUT vs CG) influences graft stability more than the sling-suture modification does, and MSS conferred no shrinkage benefit at 3 months.

## Key Contributions

- Pre-registered RCT showing the modified sling suture (MSS) does **not** reduce FGG graft shrinkage vs conventional suture.
- Identifies the gingival unit transfer (GUT) graft technique as the driver of reduced shrinkage, independent of suture technique.

## Methodology

- 60 enrolled / 52 completed; Cairo RT2/RT3 mandibular-anterior recessions; <1 mm attached gingiva.
- 4 groups (n=13): GUT+MSS, GUT+CS, CG+MSS, CG+CS; two-stage coin-toss randomization.
- Recipient bed: MGJ horizontal + 2 vertical releasing incisions, deepithelialized; donor 1-1.5 mm graft from maxillary premolar palate (GUT includes marginal+interdental gingiva).
- Outcomes (baseline, 1, 3 mo): primary = Δ graft surface area (ImageJ photogrammetry); secondary = KTW, rGRH, rVD (acrylic stent + UNC 15 probe + digital caliper). Shrinkage% = (preop-postop)/preop ×100.
- SPSS 22; ANOVA/Tukey or Kruskal-Wallis/Dunn; repeated-measures ANOVA; Spearman correlation. Single operator.

## Results

- **KTW gain:** GUT+CS > GUT+MSS at 1 and 3 mo (p<0.05); other groups not significantly different.
- **rGRH:** decreased over time; intragroup significant only in GUT+MSS; no between-group difference.
- **rVD:** no between-group difference.
- **Graft shrinkage (ΔGSA):** decreased in all groups vs baseline (p<0.05); **CG+MSS had the greatest shrinkage** at all time points vs both GUT groups (p<0.05).
- No correlation between graft dimensional change and baseline measurements.
- **Conclusion:** keratinized tissue obtained successfully with both GUT and CG; regardless of suture, GUT shrinks less than CG; MSS gave no shrinkage benefit.
- **Living-document note:** 3-month follow-up only, single center/operator, n=13/group, retrospective trial registration.
