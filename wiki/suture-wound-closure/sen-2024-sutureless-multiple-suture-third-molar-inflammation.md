## Summary
A prospective randomized clinical trial (Goa Dental College, India) of 101 patients with moderately difficult impacted mandibular third molars (Pederson index 5–7), randomized to primary closure with silk sutures (Group A) or suture-less closure (Group B). The trial measured the postoperative inflammatory triad (pain, swelling, trismus) plus lingual nerve sensation and complications. Pain, swelling, and lingual nerve function were equivalent between groups, but the suture-less arm had significantly less trismus and fewer delayed-wound-healing cases — supporting suture-less closure as a non-inferior, possibly trismus-sparing option.

## Key Contributions
- One of the larger single-trial suture-vs-sutureless comparisons (n=101) in this surgical question.
- Isolates a trismus and delayed-healing advantage for suture-less closure while confirming equivalence for pain, swelling, and lingual nerve outcomes.

## Methodology
- Prospective RCT, lottery-based simple randomization, 1:1 to silk-suture primary closure vs suture-less.
- Inclusion: age 17–55, ASA I, asymptomatic impacted third molars, Pederson difficulty 5–7.
- Exclusion: ASA II–IV, anesthetic allergy, immunocompromise, healing-affecting medications, pregnancy/lactation, bleeding disorders.
- Primary outcomes: pain, facial swelling, trismus. Secondary: lingual nerve sensation, complications.
- Analysis: non-parametric tests + repeated-measures ANOVA, P < 0.05.

## Results
- Pain, swelling, lingual nerve function: comparable between groups.
- Trismus: significantly less in suture-less group.
- Delayed wound healing: fewer cases in suture-less group.
- Conclusion: suture-less method may reduce trismus vs multiple sutures; other inflammatory outcomes equivalent.
